# tri-boost — Methodology Review (is the spec the best approach for the aims?)

> 2026-06-22. An adversarial review of the spec: 6 first-principles red-team attacks on the load-bearing methodology decisions, a defender forced to steelman the status quo, and an adjudicated verdict. Question: does the spec lead to the BEST methodology for {most accurate, fully ≤3rd-order decomposable, fast}? The six red-team reports and the defense are in [`methodology-review/`](methodology-review/).
>
> **Headline:** largely yes — one real blind spot (the spec baked a v1 *capacity convenience*, depth = 3 splits, into the product *invariant*, when only the ≤3-distinct-feature order cap is required). Fix: make tree depth a knob defaulting to 3, plus four cheap exactness-safe fixes. No redesign.

---

> **⚖️ DECISION (2026-06-22) — depth-3 is FIXED; the depth-knob recommendation is DECLINED.** The headline recommendation below (expose tree depth as a benchmark-gated `max_depth` knob) was reviewed and **declined in favour of a simpler library**. The deciding insight: a depth-3 *ensemble* is already a *universal approximator of ≤3rd-order functions*, so greater depth adds **no representational capacity** — only a marginal, data-dependent greedy-boosting convergence-efficiency gain that does not justify the variable-depth machinery (variable-length leaf arrays, cross-level monotone bound-propagation, larger tables). Trees stay fixed `[f32; 8]` / 3-split objects (see the Remark in spec §01). The product-`w` wording correction (#3) is **applied**; the per-cell effective-support annotation (#2) and the distillation-blend default (#4) remain small, optional improvements. The verdict below is retained as the analysis of record — read every "expose depth as a knob" as "declined; depth fixed at 3."

## Verdict — is the spec largely the best methodology, or does it need material change?

**Largely the best — but with one genuine, material omission that should ship as a knob, not a default flip.** The spec is a strong, internally-coherent design whose *spine* is correct and well-defended: gradient boosting (not backfitting / penalized-fANOVA / soft-NN), oblivious symmetric trees, constant-cell leaves, accumulate-then-purify-once under a fixed reference measure, the soft-prior heredity/FAST/Sobol funnel with `InteractionPolicy` as the sole hard gate, and the typed Exact/Approximate firewall. Five of six red-teamers and the defense converge on a single substantive finding — the spec **conflates the required order cap (≤3 distinct raw features) with an incidental capacity cap (depth = 3 splits = 8 cells)** — and they are right that the conflation is real and that relaxing it is exactly ≤3rd-order decomposable. But the defense is also right that the red-team *framing* over-reaches: the inference cost is mildly negative (not neutral/positive), the accuracy payoff is dataset-dependent and ~0 on the flat data that dominates TabArena, and it carries real monotone-propagation and merged-grid costs the reports under-count. So the spec needs **material change of exactly one kind — exposing depth as a benchmark-gated knob defaulting to 3 — plus four cheap, exactness-safe fixes.** It does not need a redesign, and it does not need the default flip the reports push. The owner is *not* leaving the best methodology unbuilt; they are leaving one expressiveness knob unexposed and a handful of honesty annotations unwired.

## What is right and load-bearing (KEEP)

- **Gradient boosting as the base learner** — joint boosting survives correlated features where staged/cyclic fitting (EBM/GAMI-Tree) mis-converges (§07.1); data-adaptive thresholds give the *minimal* exact union grid; histogram split-search is row-count-independent. The penalized-fANOVA rival loses on speed and the adaptive-grid story; soft-NN breaks exactness and survives only as teacher/detector, which the spec already uses.
- **Oblivious / symmetric structure; asymmetric trees rejected** — research/01 §3 and the closability check agree obliviousness costs ≈0 Elo at the ensemble level (CatBoost ranks first untuned); asymmetric growth forfeits the branch-free 8-cell lookup, the rank-1-tensor-per-tree model, the clean joint monotone clamp, and the determinism/quantized-histogram story for ~nothing. All six steelman-and-reject it. Correct.
- **Purify-once-at-the-end, not during training** — purification is prediction-invariant (changes attribution, not the fitted function), so mid-training purification is pure waste; the lazily-refreshed running bank for *selection* (§07.4) is the correct exception. Load-bearing and right.
- **Soft-prior funnel with `InteractionPolicy` as the only hard gate** — FAST-RSS ≠ Newton gain, so any hard statistical screen would amputate real interactions (the planted-XOR test §07.9). The best-designed part of the spec.
- **Quantized i64 integer histograms** doing double duty as the ~2× speed lever *and* the bit-reproducibility mechanism (integer adds are associative ⇒ order-independent) — frontier-correct (LightGBM 4.x), and it makes the determinism [GATE] nearly free.
- **Product-`w` as the default reference measure** — `w` is attribution-only (held-out predictions are identical under any `w`); product-`w` earns the default on single-pass purify, Sobol-sum-to-1, and exact equal-split SHAP. (Only the *rationale wording* needs fixing — see below.)
- **The five-invariant firewall and complete-vs-pruned view** — Reconstruction/Mass/Purity/VarianceSum/ThreeWayEqual as build-blocking gates, with LUT-sum inference lossless only over the *complete* support and pruning a non-destructive display view. The structural defense against death-by-a-thousand-cuts. Sound.

## Genuinely suboptimal — CHANGE these (prioritized by aim-impact)

**1. Tree expressiveness: depth-as-3-splits → benchmark-gated `max_depth` knob (the headline).**
- *Current:* I1 fixes `distinct_raw == splits.len() == depth ≤ 3` (§2.5 line 153, §3 line 251); each feature splits at most once → exactly one breakpoint per axis per tree → an 8-cell box. A feature cannot repeat across levels.
- *Change:* Relax I1's *check* to `distinct_raw(splits) <= 3` (drop the `== depth` clause); add `Config.max_depth ∈ {3,4,5,6}` **defaulting to 3**; enforce the ≤3-distinct-raw budget independently in the §06.2 admissibility guard (stop forbidding re-use of an already-chosen raw feature). Widen `leaves: [f32; 8]` → `SmallVec<[f32; 64]>` and `depth: u8 ∈ 1..=6`. Allow a feature to repeat across levels, giving up to 8× finer per-axis resolution and 8× more interaction cells *within one tree* on the *same* ≤3-feature support.
- *Aim(s) improved:* **Predictiveness** — attacks the spec's own most-cited deficit ("depth-3 more aggressive than CatBoost's depth-6," design/02 line 85) at the *source*, with *bias* reduction the entire §09 booster suite cannot provide. Magnitude: dataset-dependent — meaningful on interaction-rich / ordinal-heavy data, **~0 on the flat data that dominates TabArena** (closability: "past order 3 rarely helps," within-cell curvature is the same axis). Secondary **training-speed** upside via reduced tree count, *unmeasured* and possibly negative on flat data (8× per-tree gain-scan cost, subtraction trick saves less).
- *Preserves exact ≤3rd-order decomposability?* **Yes, bit-for-bit.** fANOVA order is a property of the distinct-feature support, not depth or leaf count (§3 theorem; research/03 §1.1). The §08.2 accumulator is already grid-agnostic (broadcasts a tree cell onto every merged sub-cell, keys tensors on distinct-raw support). Purification, the five gates, Sobol, Faith-Shap all unchanged.
- *Confidence:* **high** on decomposability-safety and that the knob should exist; **medium** on the size and even sign of the net accuracy/speed win — which is precisely why it is a knob, not a default.
- *Cost:* Contained struct/type widening across §02/§06/§10; **plus two costs the reports under-count and the defense correctly flags**: (a) the §07.5 monotone bound-propagation no longer degenerate (a feature splitting twice in one tree needs cross-level bound propagation — real implementation work, currently waved away by "each constrained feature splits at most once"); (b) **path-B/merged-grid blow-up** — deeper trees realize more borders per axis, enlarging the 3-D tensors that the reports *separately* flag as an inference/memory risk (§08.2's "few hundred per axis" is the same tensor). The credibility floor (now binding across up to 64 cells) partially self-throttles the gain on thin data. **Default stays 3; raising the default above 3 is gated behind the §14 benchmark AND the monotone-propagation rewrite.**

**2. Per-cell effective support on exported tables (cheap honesty fix).**
- *Current:* Credibility floors (§07.3 step 2) bind at *train time on the 8-cell oblivious leaf*, not on the finer *merged-grid display cells* — so a displayed triple cell can be thinner than any leaf that contributed to it, showing a confident relativity backed by ~11 policies with nothing flagging it.
- *Change:* Carry a per-cell `w`-mass / effective-count alongside `EffectTable.values`, surfaced exactly like the existing display-only `SeBand`.
- *Aim improved:* **Decomposability-as-faithfulness** (the actual product requirement is trustworthy tables). *Preserves decomposability?* **Yes** — metadata, never summed into score. *Confidence:* **high.** *Cost:* trivial (the `WeightCache` already computes per-axis mass; joint count is one pass over binned data).

**3. Product-`w` rationale wording correction.**
- *Current:* §08.4/§01 claim product-`w` "dodges Hooker's extrapolation in empty regions."
- *Change:* Correct to "respects per-axis *marginal* density and stays strictly positive." Product-of-marginals weights `p(x_i)·p(x_j)` are positive on never-co-occurring combinations — that *is* the Hooker extrapolation; only `Joint`-`w` dodges it, and Laplace smoothing *deepens* the reliance. **Default unchanged** — only the false fidelity claim is removed.
- *Aim improved:* none (documentation), but it stops the spec licensing a false statement to a regulator. *Preserves decomposability?* **N/A.** *Confidence:* **high.** *Cost:* one-line edit.

**4. Distillation default blend 0.25 → ~0.5, plus a direct-vs-distilled A/B.**
- *Current:* `BlendedLoss` default `blend = 0.25` (75% teacher weight); distillation framed as "highest-upside new lever."
- *Change:* Raise the default toward 0.5; add a held-out-deviance A/B (the teacher is fit anyway, so nearly free) and keep the distilled student only when it wins. An order-capped student can already *represent* the teacher's ≤3 projection; distillation's real benefit is **denoising**, which overlaps with bagging/refit — 75% teacher weight is too aggressive a default and imports teacher tail-miscalibration on Tweedie/Gamma.
- *Aim improved:* **Predictiveness** (modest, concentrated on noisy/high-cardinality data). *Preserves decomposability?* **Yes** — target-only. *Confidence:* **medium.** *Cost:* near-zero.

## Resolve by experiment

- **The depth knob's default (3 vs 4 vs 6).** Reasoning cannot settle whether the tree-count drop offsets the 8× per-tree cost, nor whether within-cell curvature exists on the target. *Ablation:* on an interaction-rich/ordinal-heavy pricing fixture AND a flat TabArena slice, sweep `max_depth ∈ {3,4,5,6}` at matched compute; measure held-out deviance, tree count, total wall-clock, and merged-grid cell count. Promote the default above 3 only where it wins net wall-clock-to-accuracy *and* the monotone-propagation extension is implemented.
- **Fully-corrective leaf refit (keep v2/off, but benchmark first among v2 levers).** Cheap `8T`-dim ridge, exactness-trivial, shrinks tree count → smaller tables. *Reject default-on* (the reports' ask): it introduces a float-reduction solve into the deterministic path that must be made thread-count-deterministic before it can ship — work not yet written. *Ablation:* measure tree-count/accuracy win on pricing data **and** prove the determinism-gate extension, then decide promotion.
- **Merged-grid blow-up.** §08.2's "few hundred cells per axis" is *asserted*, not bounded; a hot feature split at many borders across 1000–4000 trees can approach full bin cardinality, and a triple on three hot axes is ~254³ ≈ 130 MB. *Benchmark* the cell count and purification time on a deliberately border-rich + K-bag fixture; budget it in §14. **Reject border-coarsening/snapping** — it breaks the Reconstruction gate by construction (the merged grid is the *minimal exact* grid). Sparse-tensor storage for hot triples is the exactness-preserving fallback, an engineering note, not a methodology change.
- **`HeredityMode` Strong vs Weak** (§07.10) — decide on interaction-rich data before v1 freeze; the 3rd-order heredity extension is an unvalidated extrapolation.

## Net

**Change now (high-confidence, low-cost, exactness-safe):** (1) per-cell effective-support annotation on exported tables; (2) correct the product-`w` "dodges Hooker" wording; (3) raise the distillation blend default toward 0.5 and add the direct-vs-distilled A/B; (4) relax I1's *check* to `distinct_raw <= 3` and widen the `ObliviousTree` types so depth is *expressible* — but ship `Config.max_depth` **defaulting to 3** (today's behavior).

**Gate behind experiment:** raising `max_depth`'s default above 3 (needs the §14 benchmark *and* the §07.5 monotone-propagation rewrite *and* the §08.9 merged-grid cost write-up); fully-corrective refit promotion (first v2 lever to benchmark, after its determinism extension is written); the merged-grid size/purification-time bound on adversarial fixtures.

**Explicitly reject:** the reports' framing of the depth decouple as "near-free at inference / the first move / the biggest lever" (its inference cost *is* the merged-grid blow-up they flag separately — the same tensor — and its accuracy payoff is ~0 on the flat TabArena majority); default `subsample < 1.0` (not free, compounds per-tree weakness, and incumbents default to 1.0); shipping the between-`w` drift score `D_u` as a per-table annotation (crosses the stated core/downstream boundary and forces full Joint-`w` purification on every export — keep it the internal diagnostic); and border-coarsening (firewall breach). The genuine residual gap — Higgs/Year-style ≥4-way structure — is a *bias* ceiling that no depth-within-≤3-features, no refit, no distillation, and no ensembling crosses, and the spec already discloses it honestly.

**One-line answer to the owner:** Yes — the spec is essentially the best methodology for most-accurate + exactly ≤3rd-order decomposable + fast, *once you expose tree depth as a knob (default 3) and wire the four cheap fixes above*; its one real blind spot was treating a v1 capacity convenience as a product requirement, and the fix is to make it tunable, not to flip the default or redesign anything.

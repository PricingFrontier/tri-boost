# DEFENSE OF THE SPEC — Adjudication of the Six Red-Team Reports

I read the skeleton, §06/§07/§08, the brainstorm, the gap-closing analysis, closability.md, research/01 (oblivious trees), and the accuracy-model-class research (including the actual "line 99" and the linear-leaf verdict). Below I rule on every significant challenge. The reports cluster onto one headline change and a handful of secondary ones. I take the headline seriously because five of six reports independently land on it — but I do not rubber-stamp it, and I split it into the part that survives and the part that does not.

---

## CHALLENGE 1 (headline, all six reports): Decouple depth from feature-count — allow a feature to repeat across levels, raise `max_depth` to 4–6 on ≤3 distinct features.

**Verdict: CONCEDE the principle, but DOWNGRADE to a benchmark-gated `max_depth` knob (default 3), and REBUT the "biggest unexploited lever / change it first" framing. Two reports' cost accounting is materially wrong.**

### What is genuinely conceded

The decomposability-safety argument is airtight and I will not pretend otherwise. The fANOVA order is a property of the **distinct-feature support**, full stop (research/03 §1.1; the I1 theorem). A depth-5 oblivious tree on `{age, age, region, age, vehicle}` is exactly a piecewise-constant function of 3 features → exactly ≤3rd-order. The spec's own §08.2 accumulator is already grid-agnostic: it broadcasts a tree cell onto every merged sub-cell it covers and keys tensors on distinct-raw support, **not** on "8 cells." The I1 *check* in §2.5 is `distinct_raw == depth`; relaxing the load-bearing content to `distinct_raw <= 3` (dropping the incidental `== depth` clause) does not touch purification, the five gates, Sobol, or Faith-Shap. **On decomposability the reports are correct: this is free.** And the expressiveness argument is real — depth-3-as-3-splits gives each axis exactly one breakpoint per tree, so curvature within a 3-feature cell is approximated across many trees. A deeper tree on the same support captures it in one. This is a true expressiveness gain in the same order class.

So the principle stands: **the spec conflates a required order cap with an incidental depth cap, and the incidental cap is not forced by any aim.** I concede that I1's "== depth" clause should be relaxed to "≤ 3 distinct raw" and a `max_depth` knob exposed.

### What I rebut — and this is where the reports overreach

**(a) Three reports' inference cost accounting is wrong, and they partly know it.** The tree-structure, learning-framework, and predictiveness reports variously claim "inference is neutral or positive" because "the shipped artifact is the LUT-sum table bank, not the trees" (learning-framework states this most strongly: "the 8-cell lookup is a training-time convenience, not the inference product"). **This is false on the spec's own terms and the decomposability-fidelity report inadvertently proves it.** There are *two* inference paths (§10): path A (branch-free 8-cell tree lookup) and path B (table-sum scoring). Path B's tensors are `|Ω_i|·|Ω_j|·|Ω_k|` on the **merged union grid**, and deeper trees *enlarge that grid* by realizing more distinct borders per axis. So the change is not "neutral on path B" — it makes path-B tensors bigger, which is exactly the merged-grid blow-up the speed and fidelity reports flag elsewhere. The reports cannot simultaneously claim "path B makes depth free" (to wave away the inference cost) and "merged-grid blow-up is a real under-counted risk" (challenge 6). Those are the same tensor. The honest accounting is: path A goes from 3 compares + 32 B table to up to 6 compares + 256 B table (still L1-resident — research/01 §2 confirms depth-6 = 64 floats = 256 B), and path B grows with realized-border count. Net inference is **mildly negative**, not "plausibly positive." The "positive via tree-count reduction" claim is a *conjecture* that depends entirely on the unmeasured tree-count drop.

**(b) The training-cost rebuttal the reports concede but then under-weight.** Per-level histogram build is `O(2^d · n_axes · n_bins)` for the gain scan, and the subtraction trick saves *less* as leaf count grows (the speed report admits this; the others gloss it). At depth 6 the per-tree structure search is up to 8× the depth-3 cost. The entire net-speed case rests on "fewer trees more than offsets per-tree cost" — which is **unmeasured and dataset-dependent**. On flat/main-effect data (most of TabArena, per closability §c — "order-2/3 is the sweet spot, past order 3 rarely helps") there is little within-cell curvature to capture, so deeper trees buy ~0 accuracy while costing 8× per tree. This is precisely why it must be a **benchmark-gated knob defaulting to 3**, not "the first move."

**(c) Two invariant interactions the reports under-handle.**
- **Monotone bound-propagation (§07.5).** The spec explicitly leans on "each constrained feature splits at most once" to make `intermediate`/`advanced` bound-relaxation degenerate ("`basic`-style enforcement only in v1"). If a feature can split twice in one tree, the per-level joint clamp must propagate bounds across *repeated splits on the same axis within one tree* — non-trivial, and the spec's current monotone machinery does not cover it. The speed report alone flags this; it is a real implementation cost the others ignore.
- **The credibility floor (§07.3 step 2) binds across "all 8 cells of the shared level."** At depth 6 that becomes all 64 cells, each needing `min_data_in_leaf`/`min_sum_hessian`. Deeper trees on the same data mean thinner cells → the floor will veto more levels → more early-termination. This partially *self-limits* the depth gain on exactly the thin-support/correlated data where the merged-grid blow-up is worst. The reports treat depth as monotonically beneficial; the credibility floor makes it self-throttling.

**(d) The "line 99 confirms the principle" citation is an overreach.** Three reports cite accuracy-model-class line 99 as the spec's own endorsement that ≤3-distinct-feature support keeps fANOVA at order 3 "regardless of per-feature complexity." I read line 99: it is about **linear leaves (GBDT-PL)**, where a depth-d branch carries ≤d regressors. It does establish the general support-not-representation principle — but the spec applied it *and then rejected linear leaves to research-only* because they "change the table FORM" and trip the firewall. The repeated-split case does *not* change table form (cells stay constant), so it is genuinely safer than the case line 99 discusses. But the reports should not present line 99 as the spec having already blessed depth-extension; it did not consider it.

### The steelman the reports correctly make (and I affirm)

I concede the reports correctly **reject** asymmetric/local-split trees (tree-structure and architecture both steelman-and-reject this). Asymmetric growth forfeits the branch-free path-A lookup, the rank-1-tensor-per-tree model, the clean joint monotone clamp, and the determinism/quantized-histogram story — for ~0 ensemble accuracy (research/01 §3, closability §b: oblivious ≈ leaf-wise). The reports are right that *only resolution (repeated splits), not symmetry,* should move. That is the correct, narrow concession.

### Ruling on Challenge 1

**Adopt:** relax I1's check to `distinct_raw(splits) <= 3` (drop `== depth`); add `Config.max_depth ∈ {3,4,5,6}` **defaulting to 3** (today's behavior); enforce the ≤3-distinct-raw budget independently in the §06.2 admissibility guard (stop forbidding re-use of an already-used raw feature; keep capping *distinct* count). **Gate the default change behind the §14 benchmark**, and require the monotone-propagation extension (§07.5) and the path-B/merged-grid cost (§08.9) to be addressed before raising the default above 3. **Reject** the framing that this is "the first move" or "the single biggest lever" — it is dataset-dependent (largest on interaction-rich/ordinal-heavy, ~0 on flat), it carries a real path-B/monotone/credibility cost the reports under-count, and it does **not** touch the order-3 *bias* ceiling (the actual residual on Higgs/Year). It is a real, bounded improvement worth a knob — not a default flip on faith.

## CHALLENGE 2 (predictiveness, speed): Promote fully-corrective leaf refit from v2/off to v1.5/on.

**Verdict: REBUT the promotion-to-default; CONCEDE it deserves to be the *first* v2 lever benchmarked.**

The reports are right that final fully-corrective refit is cheap (an `8T`-dim ridge/Cholesky, structure frozen → linear in leaves), exactness-trivial (only leaf scalars move; §08.3 linearity holds), and shrinks tree count → smaller tables. The spec already says all of this (§06.9, design/02 Tier-2 #17). The disagreement is *default-on vs default-off*.

I rebut default-on for a concrete reason the reports skip: **the spec's whole determinism/exactness contract.** A joint ridge solve over `8T` parameters introduces a linear-algebra solve into the deterministic path. The spec's bit-reproducibility [GATE] (byte-equality across thread counts) currently rests on integer histograms + fixed-order reduction. A Cholesky/IRLS solve is a new float-reduction surface that must be made thread-count-deterministic (fixed-order, no rayon steal-order) and proven so — the spec's §1 discipline applies but is not yet written for this path. That is real work, not free. Second: refit *interacts with early stopping* — refitting all leaves after a `best_iteration` truncation changes the loss surface the holdout was selected on; the spec's "table accumulation must use the exact `best_iteration` prefix" contract (§06.6) needs re-derivation. Neither is fatal, but both mean "promote to default-on pending one benchmark" understates the surface.

**Concede:** of the three v2 levers (Nesterov, refit, multi-Newton), refit is the cheapest and most exactness-aligned, and the spec's own fork (§06.9) already recommends revisiting it first. So: **keep default-off for v1, make it the first v2 lever the §14 benchmark evaluates, and the bar for promotion is (a) measured tree-count/accuracy win on pricing data AND (b) the determinism-gate extension written.** That is a re-ordering within v2, not a promotion to v1.5-default. The reports earn the re-ordering, not the default flip.

## CHALLENGE 3 (predictiveness): Distillation is over-prioritized; raise default `blend` from 0.25 to ~0.5; gate direct-vs-distilled per dataset.

**Verdict: CONCEDE the blend default and the per-dataset gate; REBUT the "demote from highest-upside" as already-handled.**

The predictiveness report's mechanism analysis is correct and sharp: an order-capped student can *already represent* the teacher's ≤3rd-order projection directly, so distillation's real benefit is **denoising** (lower-variance regression target), not "importing unrepresentable structure" — and that denoising overlaps with what bagging and refit already buy. The `blend=0.25` default (75% teacher weight) is genuinely aggressive: it down-weights true labels in favor of a target only as good as the teacher's own ≤3 projection, and on Tweedie/Gamma tails the teacher's miscalibration gets imported.

**Concede:** raise the `BlendedLoss` default toward `0.5` (the §05 default of 0.25 is too teacher-heavy as a *default*; 0.25 should be the explicitly-noisy-regime setting), and add the direct-vs-distilled held-out-deviance A/B as a gated step (the teacher is fit anyway, so the comparison is nearly free). These are cheap, exactness-neutral (target-only), and the evidence (META-ANOVA: student sometimes *beats* teacher but margins are thin on an already-order-capped CatBoost teacher) supports a less teacher-heavy default.

**Rebut the priority claim partially:** the spec already files distillation as v1.5, default-off, firewall-gated (§09.2, design/02 item 6 — "the highest-upside *new* lever," not "the primary bet"). "Highest-upside new lever" is a defensible characterization given it is the one lever that pulls toward the *teacher's* full ≤3 projection rather than re-deriving it. The report's substantive wins are the blend default and the A/B gate; the "demote" is mostly relabeling something the spec already scopes correctly. One agreement worth recording: the architecture report's note that a **leaf-wise/deeper teacher (LightGBM/NN)** is a more informative soft target than CatBoost (whose own ≤3 projection is closest to the student) is a fair, cheap A/B — worth testing, exactness-neutral.

## CHALLENGE 4 (speed): Default `subsample < 1.0` (e.g. 0.5–0.8) so MVS actually cuts rows out of the box.

**Verdict: REBUT.**

The speed report calls `subsample=1.0` "leaving free training speed on the table." It is not free, and the spec's default is deliberate. Three reasons:

1. **MVS at `subsample<1.0` trades split-score variance for speed, and the spec's whole accuracy posture is "depth-3 trees are already weak per-tree."** Adding row subsampling *on top of* a deliberately-weak learner compounds the per-tree variance the spec is fighting with more-trees/lower-LR. The gap-closing analysis (design/02 #13) scopes MVS as a STRONG accuracy-preserving lever *at the user's discretion*, not a default row-cut.
2. **Determinism/reproducibility surface.** `subsample<1.0` by default means every out-of-the-box model depends on the MVS draw; the spec keeps the default path maximally simple and reproducible, with sampling an opt-in knob.
3. **The "every incumbent ships row subsampling on by default" claim is false.** XGBoost `subsample` defaults to **1.0**; LightGBM `bagging_fraction` defaults to **1.0** (bagging off unless `bagging_freq>0`); CatBoost uses Bayesian bootstrap but `subsample` is not a blanket <1 default for all modes. The incumbents default to *no* row subsampling and let the user turn it on. The spec matches them.

The report's own hedge ("must be benchmarked") concedes the case. Row subsampling is correctly an opt-in knob, defaulting off. **No change.**

## CHALLENGE 5 (decomposability-fidelity): (a) correct the product-`w` "dodges Hooker" rationale; (b) promote the between-`w` drift score `D_u` to a shipped per-table annotation; (c) carry per-cell effective support on exported tables.

**Verdict: (a) CONCEDE — it's a documentation fix, not a methodology change. (b) REBUT — out of scope by the spec's stated boundary, and the report mis-frames the firewall. (c) CONCEDE — cheap, on-thesis, genuinely missing.**

**(a) The rationale wording.** The report is technically right: product-of-marginals weights `p(x_i)·p(x_j)` is strictly positive on never-co-occurring combinations — that *is* the extrapolation Hooker objected to, and Laplace smoothing (`ŵ_lap ∝ ŵ_unif + λ·ŵ_emp`) actively injects mass into empty cells. The spec's §08.4 line "respects per-axis data density (dodges Hooker's extrapolation in empty regions)" overstates it. **Concede the wording fix:** product-`w` *respects per-axis marginal density and stays positive*; it does **not** dodge joint-region extrapolation — only `Joint`-`w` does that. This is a one-line correction at §08.4/§01, and it does **not** change the default (the report agrees product-`w` earns its default place on single-pass speed, Sobol-sum-to-1, and exact equal-split SHAP). Cheap, correct, adopt.

**(b) Promote `D_u` drift to a shipped annotation.** This is the report's most ambitious ask and I rebut it as scoped. The spec deliberately files between-`w` relativity drift as "an internal diagnostic, not shipped tooling" (§08.4, brainstorm §5/hedges). The report calls this "a category error — a per-table annotation is not benchmarking." But the spec's boundary (brainstorm §8, restated three times) is explicit and broader than the report allows: *"we do not ship benchmarking / model-comparison / cost-of-the-cap tooling."* A per-table "your product-`w` and joint-`w` relativities disagree by `D_u`" score is precisely a *cost-of-the-correlation-assumption* meter — it is model-introspection tooling of exactly the class the spec rules out of the core, deferring it to the downstream fairness/audit layer (brainstorm §6: "the transparent tables *enable* an audit for free; performing one is not our job"). The mechanism is already specified to be *built early and measured internally* — so the capability exists; what the report wants is to **ship and surface** it, which crosses the stated core/downstream line. There is also a real cost the report under-states: shipping `D_u` means *every* export computes the full `Joint`-`w` purification (iterative-to-fixpoint, `max_iter=64`), not just the single-pass product-`w` — and on the K-bag ensemble path that is K joint purifications over hot-feature tensors. The report calls this "small," but it is the same merged-grid blow-up it flags as a real risk in challenge 6. **Rebut:** keep `D_u` as the specified internal diagnostic; do not promote to a shipped per-table annotation in the core. (If a future downstream audit module wants it, that is the right home.) The report's narrower point — that *if* display-pruning ever hides a high-drift-but-low-product-`w`-variance table, that is a fidelity hole — is worth a one-line note in §08.7 (pruning is by product-`w` Sobol; a table with low product-`w` variance but high `D_u` could be hidden), but that is a documented caveat, not shipped machinery.

**(c) Per-cell effective support.** This one I concede cleanly. A 3-D triple table on the union grid has many near-empty cells under correlation; the model assigns each a crisp value, purification centers it against Laplace-inflated weight, and **nothing on the exported table tells the actuary the cell rests on 11 policies.** The credibility floors (§07) bind at *train time on the 8-cell oblivious leaf*, not on the finer *merged-grid* display cells — so a displayed cell can be thinner than any leaf that contributed to it. The report is right that this is a real honesty gap, it is cheap (the `WeightCache` already computes per-axis mass; the joint per-cell count is one pass over binned data), and it is exactness-safe (metadata, never summed into score — exactly like the existing `SeBand` display-only annotation, which is the precedent that defeats the "no extra annotations" objection). **Concede:** carry a per-cell effective-count / `w`-mass alongside `EffectTable.values`, surfaced like the SE-band. This is on-thesis (the firewall philosophy is "the number deployed is the number audited" — a confident relativity on no data violates the spirit) and small. Adopt.

## CHALLENGE 6 (speed): The "few hundred merged cells per axis" assumption is asserted, not bounded — add a coarsening/quota policy; and budget purification time on border-rich fixtures.

**Verdict: CONCEDE the bound-it concern as a documentation/benchmark gap; REBUT the coarsening policy as exactness-breaking-by-default.**

The speed report is right that §08.1/§08.9's "a few hundred per axis at most" is an *assertion*. The union grid is the accumulation of distinct realized borders across all trees; for a hot feature split at many borders across 1000–4000 trees with `max_bin=254`, the union can approach full bin cardinality (~254), and a 3-D table on three hot axes is `254³ ≈ 16M` f64 cells = ~130 MB for a single triple. This is a genuine path-B inference + purification + memory cost, and — importantly — **Challenge 1 (deeper trees) makes it worse**, and the ensemble/bagging path (union across K members) makes it worse again. The reports collectively cannot wave away depth's path-B cost (challenge 1) while flagging this (challenge 6); they are the same tensor. So I concede: the spec should (i) **state the merged-grid cell bound as a measured quantity** with a deliberately border-rich + K-bag fixture in §14, and (ii) **document the worst-case 3-D tensor memory** rather than assert it away.

But I **rebut the proposed fix** — "a coarsening / threshold-quota policy that snaps near-coincident realized borders." Snapping realized borders is **exactly the firewall violation the spec exists to prevent**: the merged grid is the *minimal exact* grid (§08.1 — "a coarser one cannot represent some tree losslessly"); coarsening it breaks the Reconstruction gate and flips the model to `Approximate`. The spec's own §3 lists "true zero-tolerance bit-equality" as the contract. A coarsening policy with "an exactness-preserving error bound" is a contradiction — any border-merge that changes a cell value fails Reconstruction by construction. The report's *alternative* fallback ("flip to a sparse tensor representation above a cell threshold") is the right shape and is exactness-preserving (sparse storage of the same values), but that is an *implementation* optimization for §08/§10, not a methodology change — it can be noted as a future engineering item without touching the contract. **Ruling:** concede "measure and document the merged-grid size on adversarial fixtures, and budget purification time in §14"; reject "coarsen/snap borders" (firewall breach); note "sparse tensor storage for hot triples" as a non-binding engineering option.

## Items the reports correctly affirm (no change — recorded so the adjudicator doesn't re-litigate)

- **Oblivious/symmetric structure kept; asymmetric trees rejected.** All six steelman-and-reject asymmetric growth (research/01 §3; closability §b: oblivious ≈ leaf-wise at ensemble level; forfeits path-A, rank-1 tensor, joint clamp, determinism). Affirmed.
- **Gradient boosting as the base learner** over backfitting / penalized-fANOVA / GAMI / soft-NN (learning-framework, architecture). Correct: joint boosting survives correlation where staged/cyclic fitting mis-converges (GAMI-Tree lesson, §07.1); data-adaptive thresholds give the minimal exact union grid; row-count-independent histogram speed. The penalized-fANOVA rival loses on speed and the adaptive-grid story; soft-NN breaks exactness and survives only as teacher/detector (which the spec already uses). Affirmed.
- **Purify-once-at-the-end, not during training** (learning-framework). Correct and load-bearing: purification is prediction-invariant (changes attribution, not the fitted function), so mid-training purification is pure waste; the running purified bank for *selection* (§07.4) is the correct lazy exception. Affirmed.
- **The soft-prior heredity/FAST/Sobol funnel with `InteractionPolicy` as the sole hard gate** (learning-framework: "best-designed part"). FAST-RSS ≠ Newton gain, so a hard screen would amputate real interactions (planted-XOR test §07.9). Affirmed. (Note the open `HeredityMode::Strong`-vs-`Weak` fork §07.10 should be benchmark-decided before v1 freeze — the spec already flags this.)
- **Product-`w` as the *default*** (decomposability-fidelity, predictiveness): `w` is attribution-only, identical held-out predictions under any `w`; product-`w` earns default on single-pass purify, Sobol-sum-to-1, exact equal-split SHAP. Affirmed (only the *rationale wording* is conceded above, not the default).
- **Single shared `w` across ensemble members; Joint-`w` skips (not fails) VarianceSum; "interventional" SHAP labelling; complete-vs-pruned view** (decomposability-fidelity rules these "hold"). Affirmed.
- **Quantized i64 histograms + fixed-order reduce** as the dual speed/reproducibility mechanism (speed: "frontier-correct"). Affirmed.
- **Additive depth-1 warm-start backbone (architecture challenge C)**: exactness-preserving, cheap, sharpens mains and frees the 3-feature budget. This is a *plausible cheap add* the spec gestures at via `smoothing_rounds` but doesn't commit. I rate it a **minor, defensible v1.5 experiment** (smaller and surer than Nesterov), not a required change — joint boosting from `f0` already fits mains, and the warm-start's benefit is "table stability / budget allocation," which is real but second-order. Worth a benchmark, not a contract change.

## Bottom line for the adjudicator

**Adopt (low-cost, exactness-safe, earned):**
1. Relax I1's check to `distinct_raw <= 3` (drop `== depth`) and add `Config.max_depth ∈ {3..6}` **defaulting to 3**, gated behind the §14 benchmark *and* the §07.5 monotone-propagation extension + §08.9 path-B cost write-up. (Challenge 1 — concede principle, reject default-flip and the "biggest lever / first move" framing; the inference cost is mildly negative, not neutral/positive — the reports double-count path B.)
2. Correct the §08.4/§01 product-`w` rationale wording: "respects per-axis marginal density and stays positive," not "dodges Hooker extrapolation." (Challenge 5a.)
3. Carry per-cell effective support/mass on exported `EffectTable`s, surfaced like the existing `SeBand`. (Challenge 5c.)
4. Raise `BlendedLoss` default toward 0.5 and add a direct-vs-distilled held-out-deviance A/B. (Challenge 3.)
5. Document the merged-grid cell count as a *measured* bound on border-rich + K-bag fixtures and budget purification time in §14; note sparse-tensor storage for hot triples as a non-binding engineering option. (Challenge 6 — concede measure-it, reject border-coarsening as a firewall breach.)

**Reject (marginal, invariant-adjacent, or already-scoped):**
- Default `subsample<1.0` (Challenge 4) — not free; incumbents default to 1.0; compounds per-tree weakness.
- Promote fully-corrective refit to v1.5/default-on (Challenge 2) — keep v2/default-off; concede only that it should be the *first* v2 lever benchmarked, after the determinism-gate extension is written.
- Ship the `D_u` drift score as a per-table annotation (Challenge 5b) — crosses the stated core/downstream boundary (it is cost-of-the-cap audit tooling) and forces full Joint-`w` purification on every export; keep it the specified internal diagnostic.
- Border-coarsening/snapping (Challenge 6) — breaks the Reconstruction gate by construction.

**The one thing the reports collectively get most wrong:** they treat the depth decouple as "near-free at inference, the first move." It is neither. Its inference cost is the merged-grid/path-B blow-up they flag *separately* as a real risk — the same tensor — and its accuracy payoff is dataset-dependent and ~0 on the flat data that dominates TabArena, while costing up to 8× per-tree training and a monotone-propagation rewrite. It is a legitimate **knob** worth shipping at default-3 and benchmarking — not a default flip, and not the headline accuracy story. The actual residual gap (Higgs/Year, genuine ≥4-way structure) is a *bias* ceiling that no depth-within-≤3-features, no refit, no distillation, and no ensembling crosses — and the spec already discloses that honestly.

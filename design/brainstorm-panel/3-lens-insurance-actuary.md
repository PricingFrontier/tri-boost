# pattern-boost — Brainstorm from the Insurance Pricing Actuary's Lens

## 1. Thesis / the bet

The bet is that pattern-boost should be designed not as "an explainable GBM that happens to support Poisson," but as **a rating-engine that emits a filed rate plan as its native output** — where the depth-3 oblivious + ≤3rd-order fANOVA structure is exactly the structure of a multiplicative tariff (base rate × 1-D relativities × pairwise/triple rating cells). My core claim: the *entire competitive advantage* over a GLM is the model's ability to discover, credibly fit, and **purify** genuine 2-way and 3-way rating cells that an actuary would otherwise hand-build via cross-tabulation — while the *entire competitive advantage* over an unconstrained GBM is that what comes out is a balance-corrected, monotone, smooth, exposure-weighted set of relativity tables a pricing committee and a regulator can sign. So I'd build the pipeline around **frequency-severity-first** (two tariffs, the way real rate plans are structured), make **global balance (A/E=100%) a default-on invariant** folded into `f₀`, and treat the **rating-table export as a first-class, tested artifact** — not a `.predict()` afterthought. Tweedie is the pragmatic single-model fallback, not the headline.

## 2. Components

### A. Objective / workflow layer

**A1. Frequency-severity (Poisson × Gamma) as the default pricing workflow.** [BOTH] Two separate models: Poisson-log frequency on counts with `offset = log(exposure)`, Gamma-log severity on positive incurred amounts weighted by claim count, combined to pure premium `μ_freq × μ_sev`. Mechanism: per `04-pricing` §5 and the v1.5 "Frequency-severity twin" technique, this isolates each peril component so counteracting factors (e.g. young drivers: high frequency, *low* severity) stay visible instead of cancelling to "insignificant" under a single Tweedie. *Explainability win:* you export **two** readable tariffs, exactly how a traditional rate plan is laid out, and each table answers "why" at the peril level. *Predictiveness:* each model cuts through the other's noise. **Tradeoff:** two models to build/validate/govern, needs the freq/sev data split, and the combined pure-premium's variance decomposition is no longer a single clean fANOVA (you have two). I accept that — actuaries already think in two tariffs.

**A2. Tweedie(p) single pure-premium model as the supported fallback.** [PREDICTIVENESS] Ship it (p default 1.5, hessian needs no clamp on 1<p<2) for thin data, short timelines, or perils where freq/sev split is unavailable. *Tradeoff:* bakes in freq/sev co-movement; I'd document it as the "fast path," not the recommended one.

**A3. Exposure/offset as a first-class argument, not a weight hack.** [BOTH] A `exposure=` convenience that sets per-row `offset = log(exposure)` added to the raw score before the inverse link every iteration (XGBoost `base_margin` / LightGBM `init_score` route), with init `F₀ = log(Σwy / Σwe)`. *Why it's load-bearing:* it's what makes `e⁰ = 1.000` the base level and anchors the whole relativity interpretation. Also enables **boost-on-top-of-an-existing-GLM** (CANN-style residual boosting, v1.5) — a huge adoption lever: "keep your filed GLM, let pattern-boost add audited interaction cells on the residual." *Tradeoff:* none structurally; it's a v1 must-have.

### B. The economics-shaping constraint layer (this is where a GBM becomes a *rate plan*)

**B4. Monotone constraints, hard, via per-level joint leaf-clamp.** [BOTH] Premium must not fall as risk rises — non-negotiable for filing and for the "no lower premium for worse claim history" failure the literature flags. Mechanism (per `4-constraints` §monotone): not XGBoost's per-path midpoint recursion, but **joint pairwise constraints across the 8-cell shared-split table** (CatBoost-style), checked on the 2nd-order leaf weight `w = −G/(H+λ)`. *Explainability:* monotone 1-D shape functions are what actuaries expect and what regulators approve. *Predictiveness:* <0.2% AUC cost on large data, sometimes *improves* generalization; wrong-*sign* costs 2.5–21.2%, so I'd make it a `name→sign` dict, never positional. **Tradeoff/quirk:** an entire level's gain can go to −∞ if no monotone-valid shared split exists → the build must gracefully **terminate the tree early at depth <3** (a legitimate lower-order, fANOVA-friendly outcome, not an error). And I'd document loudly that monotonicity holds on the *total* score and the 1-D table, **not on each interaction cell in isolation** after purification.

**B5. Interaction constraints + `max_interaction_order` (the GAM↔GA2M↔order-3 dial).** [BOTH] The headline structure lever. Stricter-than-XGBoost **whole-tree** semantics: a tree's ≤3 features must lie *jointly within one allowed group*, so "permitted interactions" exactly equals "tables that can appear." Expose `max_interaction_order ∈ {1,2,3}`: 1 = pure additive GAM (regulator-facing "no interactions" mode), 2 = GA2M, 3 = full. *Explainability:* this is how you keep the table set readable and exclude regulator-prohibited interactions (e.g. forbidding a proxy pairing) "even if they perform well." *Predictiveness:* sanctioned interactions still get fit. **Tradeoff:** level-3 candidate starvation → again, graceful early termination.

**B6. Smoothness / credibility of rating factors.** [BOTH] Three native levers: `min_sum_hessian_in_leaf` as the **exposure/credibility floor per cell** (no rating cell fires on a handful of policy-years), `path_smooth` (parent-shrinkage of low-exposure cells), and merged-threshold table granularity. *Explainability:* smooth, credible relativities that don't whipsaw between adjacent age bands. **Tradeoff:** I would **not** put in-training Whittaker–Henderson/ICEnet graduation on the default path — those break exactness (see §4). Smoothness on the default path comes from credibility floors + bagging, and *graduation is an explicit opt-in post-export mode* with a "no longer bit-exact" stamp.

### C. Calibration & the BALANCE property (the actuary's non-negotiable)

**C7. Global balance / mean re-anchoring, default-ON, folded into `f₀`.** [BOTH] Deviance-trained GBMs lose the GLM canonical-link guarantee `Σμ̂ = Σy` (Denuit 2021) — a model that doesn't reproduce the portfolio's total expected losses is *unfileable*. Fix (v1): solve one scalar `δ = log(Σwy / Σw·e·exp(F))` in closed form for log-link, fold into `f₀`. *Explainability:* only the constant changes, losslessness re-asserts trivially. *Predictiveness:* corrects systematic mean bias. **Tradeoff:** none — this is the cheapest, most important post-step in the system.

**C8. Local autocalibration (`autocalibrate=True`) — declared SEPARATELY from the tables.** [PREDICTIVENESS] Denuit–Charpentier–Trufin `π_BC = E[Y | π̂(X)]` restores *local* balance and improves lift. **Critical fault line:** this is a nonlinear 1-D warp of the aggregate score, so `recalib(Σ tables) ≠ Σ tables` — it **breaks the additive decomposition.** Architectural rule: ship it as a deployment-time calibration curve sitting where the link sits, *never re-decomposed into the rating tables.* Keep exact tables for `π̂`; the local curve is a documented score→price map. **Tradeoff:** exactness vs local balance — I let the actuary choose per filing, and default it OFF.

**C9. A-vs-E / balance report — and the differentiator: balance PER TABLE.** [EXPLAINABILITY] Exposure-weighted reliability diagram, global and per-decile A/E, Lorenz/concentration curves. The unique edge: run A/E **per main effect and per interaction cell** — something black-box GBMs structurally cannot do. This is the single best "why trust this rate plan" artifact.

### D. Evaluation suite (pricing-specific, not ML-generic)

**D10.** [EXPLAINABILITY/trust] Per-objective **deviance** as the default training metric (Poisson/Gamma/Tweedie — strictly proper for the mean; RMSE is not), plus **lift / equal-exposure quantile plots, double-lift charts, Gini/Lorenz, and the ordered (economic) Gini** (Frees–Meyers–Cummings, ordering by relativity `score/price` along a cumulative-premium axis — the actuarially correct model-comparison metric with an asymptotic difference test). **Tradeoff:** none; this is table-stakes for a pricing audience and a credibility signal that the authors "get" insurance.

### E. Distributional output for risk & reserving

**E11. Quantile / pinball heads + conformal wrappers.** [PREDICTIVENESS] For severity tail / VaR / large-loss capping and reserving, ship quantile regression (v1.5) and **split-conformal / CQR** intervals (v1.5, native, preserves exactness) for guaranteed-coverage prediction intervals without distributional assumptions. `LogLinQuantile` respects the multiplicative scale. **Tradeoff:** raw multi-quantile heads "change the table form" (each quantile is its own table set / can cross); conformal sits *outside* the table decomposition as a wrapper, so I prefer **point-tariff tables + conformal interval wrapper** over distributional heads on the audited path. Distributional heads (NGBoost/LSS) are a v2 research lane for reserving, explicitly flagged as a *different* artifact.

### F. Fairness / proxy-discrimination

**F12. Discrimination-free pricing (marginalization) + feature-level proxy audit.** [EXPLAINABILITY] The v1.5 Lindholm-style marginalization provably removes proxy discrimination and preserves exactness. More importantly, the **transparent tables themselves are the primary proxy-detection tool** (Prince & Schwarcz) — an actuary can *read* whether `occupation × postcode` is reconstructing a protected attribute. Multicalibration for protected subgroups is v2 and **breaks exactness on the deployed predictor**, so same treatment as C8: separate, declared, off-by-default.

### G. The rating-table EXPORT (the actual deliverable)

**G13. A versioned, human- and regulator-readable rating-table artifact.** [EXPLAINABILITY] This is the product. Format: `f₀` → **base rate** (the `1.000` anchor); each 1-D table → a named relativity table with the **base level row = 1.000** and other levels as `exp(f_i)` multiplicative relativities; 2-D/3-D tables → labelled interaction-rating cells, each sorted/gated by **purified Sobol variance share** so near-zero tables are hidden; columns annotated with **exposure and A/E per cell**. JSON-style contract (never pickle), stable, with the reference measure `w` stamped on every table. Scoring from the tables is a sum of lookups, *bit-for-bit equal to the ensemble* (the tested losslessness invariant). **Tradeoff:** none — but it must carry the caveats (interventional-SHAP semantics, monotonicity-on-total-only, which `w`).

## 3. Highest-leverage ideas (from my lens)

1. **Frequency-severity-first, with the export being two tariffs.** This single design choice aligns pattern-boost with how rate plans are actually structured and filed, and it's where the model beats a GLM (credible auto-discovered interaction cells per peril).
2. **Balance as a default invariant (global, in `f₀`) + per-table A/E reporting.** Fixing the GBM balance violation *by construction* and surfacing balance per rating cell is the feature that converts actuarial skepticism into trust — and it's nearly free.
3. **The `max_interaction_order` dial (GAM → GA2M → order-3) as a filing strategy, not just a hyperparameter.** Lets a carrier file the additive GAM where regulators demand it and unlock interaction cells where they're allowed — same engine, same audited pipeline.
4. **Purified Sobol variance as the table-selection AND export-ranking primitive.** Free, exact, sums to ~1 under the product measure; it answers "which of my hundreds of possible 3-D cells actually matter" and drives both heredity-based train-time admission and explain-time pruning.
5. **Boost-on-top-of-the-incumbent-GLM (offset/CANN).** "Keep your filed model; we add audited residual structure" is the lowest-friction path into a conservative, regulated buyer.

## 4. What I would explicitly NOT do (and why)

- **Default Tweedie over freq/sev.** It hides counteracting risk factors and collapses two readable tariffs into one less-diagnostic table. Keep it as the fallback, label it so.
- **Fold any local/nonlinear calibration into the exported tables.** Autocalibration, multicalibration, Beta/Venn-ABERS all warp the aggregate score → `g(Σf_u) ≠ Σg(f_u)`. They live *outside* the decomposition as declared deployment curves, off by default. Only affine logit warps (Platt/temperature) fold in exactly.
- **In-training graduation / smoothness penalties on the audited path.** Whittaker–Henderson is lossy and ICEnet is NN-only; post-export graduation is an opt-in mode with a "not bit-exact" stamp. Default smoothness = credibility floors + bagging.
- **Linear/piecewise-linear leaves and distributional heads on the default path.** They turn rating *tables* into rating *functions* / per-quantile table sets — a product decision, not a default. Point tariffs + conformal interval wrapper keeps the audited artifact a clean lookup table.
- **Post-hoc SHAP/PDP/ALE machinery.** All are closed-form table reads here; running TreeSHAP would be slower *and* could silently disagree (path-cover ≠ conditional). I'd expose "interventional SHAP," "PDP," "ICE" as thin views over the purified tables, and keep stock TreeSHAP only as a test oracle. ALE is a redundant artifact whose tables aren't the deployable ones — skip.
- **Run-of-the-mill ML metrics as the headline.** RMSE/accuracy are not proper for these means and not what a pricing committee reads. Deviance + lift + ordered-Gini + A/E lead; everything else is secondary.

# Gaps the 5 blueprints under-covered (high-value for the dual aim + insurance rating)

The blueprints converged hard on a shared core (FAST/heredity interaction selection, reference-measure purification, exact-SHAP-from-tables, frequency-severity, balance/autocalibration, monotone/interaction constraints, LUT inference, quantized histograms, the order-3 diagnostic, inner/outer-bag credibility bands). Below are HIGH-VALUE items from the 229-inventory and `8-gaps-and-frontier.md` that **none adequately developed** — each a concrete adoptable component, not a restatement of the consensus.

- **Calibration Bands for the EDF (Tweedie/Poisson/Gamma), arXiv:2503.18896** — Every blueprint ships an A/E reliability diagram and a global-balance fix, but none gives a *statistical test* for whether the model is actually auto-calibrated. The calibration-band method puts honest confidence bands on the A-vs-E curve for EDF responses, so "is the balance correction significant / is this thin cell mis-calibrated?" becomes a hypothesis test, not eyeballing. This is the missing rigor layer under the reliability-diagram bullet (B5's "ship the order-3 price tag" needs *this* to size its CIs honestly). Serves EXPLAINABILITY/trust. Pairs naturally with the per-table A/E that Blueprint 3 proposed but couldn't quantify.

- **Multicalibration-as-boosting with convergence theory (Globus-Harris 2023; Jin 2025; Tax 2026)** — All blueprints mention multicalibration only to *exile* it (breaks exactness on the deployed predictor, off-by-default). None notes it can run *as a boosting subroutine* with finite-iteration guarantees — i.e. enforce per-subgroup balance *during* training rather than as a post-hoc warp. Worth a research lane because the in-processing version could fold subgroup balance into the additive structure (each subgroup-correction is itself a low-order table) instead of a non-additive response-scale warp. Serves CALIBRATION+FAIRNESS; the open question is whether the correction stays ≤3-order.

- **Conformal prediction specifically for Tweedie/frequency-severity (arXiv:2507.06921, 2503.03659, 2307.13124) + conditional-coverage diagnostics (2512.11779)** — Blueprints invoke "split-conformal/CQR preserves exactness" generically, but the Tweedie loss and the *compound* freq×severity target need bespoke nonconformity scores (the response is zero-inflated and right-skewed; marginal coverage on the compound loss ≠ coverage on each head). And critically: **conditional-coverage diagnostics** to detect where intervals silently fail on thin risk cells — exactly the cells an actuary distrusts. None of the blueprints flagged that marginal conformal coverage is near-useless per-cell without this validation layer. Serves PREDICTIVENESS/uncertainty for reserving and large-loss capping.

- **InstaSHAP + Möbius/n-Shapley/Faith-Shap unifying theorem (arXiv:2502.14177, 2209.04012, 2203.00870) as a provable headline, not just an O(1) trick** — Blueprints 2/4 correctly say SHAP is a table read, but stop at the Bordt-von Luxburg equal-split heuristic. The stronger, newer result is that a *purified order-3 additive model simultaneously IS* the unique fANOVA decomposition, the exact n-Shapley/Faith-Shap explanation up to order 3, AND the Möbius transform. That lets tri-boost claim **exact Faith-Shap interaction indices** (resolving SHAP-interaction non-uniqueness) for free — a real theoretical differentiator no blueprint asserted. Tensor-network exact-Shapley (2510.22138) is the alternative engine connecting the fANOVA tensor to Shapley-Taylor. Serves EXPLAINABILITY.

- **ANOVA-BART as the principled per-cell-uncertainty head (arXiv:2510.00545)** — Blueprint 2 name-drops it; none develops it. It is the *only* method giving genuine **per-table-cell posterior credible intervals** under the same identifiability constraint tri-boost enforces — strictly better than the inner/outer-bag SE bands (which are bootstrap annotations, not posteriors) and than SGLB/NGBoost/PGBM (per-row, not per-cell). For "how credible is this 3-way relativity in a one-claim-wide cell," this is the right long-term answer. Serves EXPLAINABILITY+uncertainty; research-tier (different fitting machinery).

- **Benchmark yardstick discipline: TabArena + RealMLP/TabM defaults + TabPFN-v2 baseline (2506.16791, 2407.04491, Nature TabPFN-v2)** — Every blueprint asserts "match XGBoost/LightGBM" but none specifies *what to benchmark against or how to seed defaults*. Three concrete actions missing: (a) benchmark on the **living TabArena leaderboard** (GBDTs no longer strictly win — TabM/RealMLP top it), so "parity" must be re-defined against current SOTA; (b) **seed v1 hyperparameter defaults from RealMLP's meta-tuned GBDT defaults** rather than inventing them; (c) treat **TabPFN-v2 as the small-portfolio baseline** tri-boost must justify itself against (insurance portfolios are often small/thin). This is the missing evaluation-design backbone. Serves PREDICTIVENESS credibility.

- **Hyperparameter-search strategy as a first-class component** — Strikingly absent from all five. The depth-3 cap *changes the tuning surface*: learning-rate × n-trees trades off differently (weaker per-tree learner → more, lower-LR trees), and `max_bin`/credibility-floor/interaction-order interact. None proposed a search protocol (e.g. the standard staged search, or warm-starting from RealMLP defaults + early-stopping on deviance). For a deployable tool this is table-stakes. Serves PREDICTIVENESS+usability.

- **Data/missingness/leakage handling as a designed subsystem** — Blueprints mention the reserved-bin / learned-default-direction missing strategy in passing, but none treats the *full data pipeline*: the **learned default split direction** (XGBoost sparsity-aware) vs CatBoost `nan_mode`, **new/unseen categorical levels at score time** (collapse-to-base, critical for postcodes), **rare-level collapsing** (`min_data_per_group`), and the **train/score binning-grid persistence** that makes missing-handling reproducible. For a *filed rating table*, "what happens to a policy with a missing or never-seen value" is an audit question, not an afterthought. Serves ROBUSTNESS+EXPLAINABILITY.

- **Deployment/compilation of the *tables* (TreeLUT FPGA'25, arXiv:2501.01511; CatBoost RVV, 2405.11062)** — Blueprint 4 covers the LUT-sum *inference kernel* but none addresses **shipping the rating tables as the deployable artifact into production scoring engines** (sub-microsecond LUT scoring, the FPGA/RVV hardware path, and emitting tables as SQL/lookup-join artifacts a policy-admin system can execute without the model). Since the tables *are* the product, their compilation/export format (beyond JSON) is a load-bearing deliverable, not a moot inference-compiler question. Serves DEPLOYABILITY.

- **Zero-inflated Tweedie with learned q↔μ link (So-Valdez, arXiv:2406.16206)** — Blueprints default to plain Tweedie(1.5) or freq×severity. For *extremely* zero-inflated P&C books, the two-part/ZITw objective with a *learned* zero-probability-to-mean relationship beats both and is a concrete g/h to implement — a predictiveness lever on exactly the hardest pricing data that no blueprint costed. Serves PREDICTIVENESS (research-tier objective).

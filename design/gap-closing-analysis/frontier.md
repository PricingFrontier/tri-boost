# Frontier scan: new gap-closers for a depth-3 oblivious, exactly-decomposable booster (2023–2026)

Your docs already inventory the soft/neural oblivious family (NODE, GRANDE, TEL, NODE-GAM), GAMI-Tree, fully-corrective/accelerated boosting, FAST, and stacking, and correctly route the soft/oblique/linear-leaf ones to "breaks-exactness." Below are techniques that are **new relative to those docs** or that recast a covered idea in a directly adoptable way. For each: what it is, exactness verdict against I1/I2, gap-closing potential, and adopt-vs-research.

## 1. Distillation FROM an unconstrained teacher INTO the decomposition — the biggest under-covered lever

This is your sharpest question, and the 2024 literature answers it directly and favorably.

**META-ANOVA (Lee, Kim, et al., arXiv:2408.00973, Aug 2024)** transforms *any* trained black box (XGBoost, RF, DNN) into a functional ANOVA model, with a provably-consistent interaction-**screening** statistic so higher-order terms can be admitted without the C(n,k) blowup. The screening statistic is `I(j) = E[Var{D_j f₀ | X_j}]` — a partial-derivative/finite-difference test that, when ≈0, lets you drop *all* interactions containing `j` at once (Apriori-style, with a heredity-like ancestor filter). The headline result is exactly the one you need: distilling the teacher costs almost nothing in accuracy — Calhousing MSE 0.164→0.165 (from XGBoost), Abalone 0.432→**0.427** (it *beat* the DNN teacher), German-credit AUROC 0.787→0.778, Letter 0.996→0.994. **Verdict: the recipe is adoptable and the most promising single gap-closer.** Concretely for tri-boost: fit an unconstrained XGBoost/LightGBM teacher, then train the depth-3 oblivious booster against the teacher's *soft outputs* (its margin) instead of (or blended with) the hard target. This is pure functional-gradient regression onto a smoother surface — it touches only the loss target, never tree shape, so **I1/I2 are untouched and the result stays exactly decomposable**. The ceiling is principled: the student can match the teacher's *≤3rd-order projection* exactly; whatever the teacher earns from genuine ≥4-way structure is the irreducible cap, and distillation is the cleanest way to recover *everything except* that. Caveat the docs already flag — a *surrogate of tri-boost* is pointless; here the teacher is the black box and the student is tri-boost, which is the useful direction. **Adopt as a v1.5/research training mode** (teacher-distillation loss), behind the exactness firewall (the distilled model is still bit-exact to its own tables; only its *fidelity to the teacher* is <1).

A second, actuarial-flavored confirmation: **Maillart & Robert (2024, Annals of Actuarial Science)** distill an additive tree ensemble into a GLM/GAM via the tree's splits, reaching parity with EBM and spline-GAMs. Same lesson, weaker mechanism (it distills *structure*, not soft labels) — useful corroboration, not a better recipe.

## 2. Higher-order interaction *selection* — the right triples without enumeration

Your interaction funnel (heredity → FAST-RSS → post-purify Sobol) is sound but FAST is a 2-way screen. Two 2022–2024 methods give a principled triple-selector:

- **SIAN — Sparse Interaction Additive Networks (Enouen & Liu, NeurIPS 2022, arXiv:2209.09326)** runs **Feature Interaction Detection (FIS)** on a quickly-trained dense net (gradient/Hessian-interaction statistics), then selects a *sparse* set of pairs/triples/higher via a heredity-constrained search, and only then fits an additive network over the survivors. SIAN-5 (order-5) and **NODE-GA3M** (NODE-GAM extended to triples) are the relevant data points: NODE-GA3M improves Housing 0.222→**0.175 (21.2%)** over the order-2 SOTA, and SIAN tops the additive-model field (avg rank 3.00 vs NODE-GA2M's 3.71 across 7 datasets). **Takeaway:** the jump from order-2 to order-3 is empirically large on spatial/structured data — direct evidence your order-3 extension over EBM is worth real accuracy, not just a story. The FIS *detector* is adoptable as a soft prior feeding your heredity allow-list (it's pre-training analysis, exactness-neutral); the SIAN/NODE-GA3M *models* themselves are soft/neural and **break exactness** (don't port them). **Adopt the detector idea; cite the models as validation.**
- **NID/PID interaction detectors** (the baselines META-ANOVA compares to, AUROC ~0.95) are alternative gradient-based pair/triple screens — interchangeable with FIS as a better-than-FAST front-end. Research-grade ranking aid.

## 3. Identifiable / unique decomposition learning — ANOVA-TPNN, ANOVA-NODE

**ANOVA-TPNN (Park et al., 2025, arXiv:2502.15215)** and **ANOVA-NODE** (OpenReview) attack the *non-uniqueness* of fANOVA components by building tensor-product bases that satisfy the uniqueness/zero-mean constraint *by construction*, giving stable components across re-runs. You already solve this differently and exactly (Lengerich purification under a fixed reference measure), so these are **not adoptable** (they're soft neural learners → break exactness). Their value: they independently confirm your reference-measure decision is the load-bearing identifiability choice, and TPNN's "constrain at construction" is conceptually the neural mirror of your purify-at-export. **Research-only / motivation.**

## 4. GRANDE's straight-through trick — confirmed transferable seed, still not adopt-as-is

The 2024 GRANDE detail (ICLR 2024, arXiv:2309.17130) reconfirms what your docs say: the STE (soft-train, **hard axis-aligned inference**) is the one transferable primitive, but GRANDE's *instance-wise leaf weighting* and *non-oblivious* nodes break I1/I2. The genuinely novel tri-boost research item — **STE-trained, oblivious, global-leaf** trees — would preserve exactness and could close part of the "weak-per-tree" gap via gradient-trained split *placement*. **Research-grade**, unchanged from your docs but now with a 2024 SOTA-vs-GBDT result behind it.

## 5. What did NOT yield a new gap-closer

TabArena (arXiv:2506.16791) confirms the landscape but not a technique: CatBoost leads single-model tuned; **post-hoc ensembling** (weighted model-soup) is what lifts the leaders — which *reinforces* your bagging/model-soup-on-tables plan (linear, exact) as the right variance lever. Fully-corrective/totally-corrective boosting (CGBoost/RGF/FCGB) surfaced again with no 2024 advance beyond what your docs hold; still the best *exact* tree-count reducer. Knowledge-distillation-with-soft-labels generically (the KD literature) adds nothing beyond META-ANOVA's tabular instantiation.

## Bottom line — adopt vs research

- **Adopt (high gap-closing): teacher-distillation training** (META-ANOVA-style soft-label fit of tri-boost to an unconstrained GBM) — exactness-preserving, recovers everything but the ≥4-way residual, the single best lever.
- **Adopt (selection): a gradient-based triple detector** (FIS/NID-style) as a soft prior into your heredity funnel, supplementing 2-way FAST.
- **Research/validation only: SIAN, NODE-GA3M, ANOVA-TPNN/-NODE, GRANDE-STE-oblivious** — they prove order-3 pays (NODE-GA3M +21%) and prove identifiability matters, but their learners are soft/neural and break I2; mine the recipes, not the code.

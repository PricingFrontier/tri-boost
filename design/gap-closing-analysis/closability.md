# REALITY CHECK: How closable is the predictiveness gap for a depth-3 oblivious, order-3 model?

**Bottom line up front:** the gap is real but modest, and on TabArena-like data it is mostly closable. The best evidence says order-2 additive models already sit a small-but-consistent distance behind unconstrained GBDTs; extending to **exact order-3** plausibly recovers a meaningful fraction of what remains; and the *oblivious-vs-leafwise* penalty is near-zero. The honest expected residual gap for pattern-boost vs the best of XGBoost/LightGBM/CatBoost is **small on most datasets and large on a minority with genuine high-order structure** — which is exactly the EBM bet, extended one order.

## (a) Additive/pairwise GAMs vs unconstrained GBMs — the gap is small but real

The foundational numbers are reassuring. On Caruana et al.'s pneumonia benchmark, plain GAM hits AUC 0.854 and **GA2M (pairwise) 0.857**, *beating* random forest (0.846) and logistic regression (0.843) — i.e., adding pairwise interactions to an additive model already reaches or exceeds black-box accuracy there ([Caruana 2015](https://www.microsoft.com/en-us/research/wp-content/uploads/2017/06/KDD2015FinalDraftIntelligibleModels4HealthCare_igt143e-caruanaA.pdf)). The original GA2M paper reports pairwise terms giving an **average ~34% RMSE reduction over plain GAM**, recovering most of the GAM→full-model gap ([Lou et al. 2013](https://www.cs.cornell.edu/~yinlou/papers/lou-kdd13.pdf)).

But this is dataset-dependent, and on a broad modern suite the gap is visible. The single most decision-relevant number I found: on **TabArena (51 curated datasets)**, EBM (pairwise GA2M) pays roughly **~160 Elo points versus LightGBM** for its glass-box constraint ([TabArena, 2025](https://arxiv.org/html/2506.16791v4); [EBM topic summary](https://www.emergentmind.com/topics/explainable-boosting-machines)). That is not catastrophic — EBM is on the inference-time Pareto front and beats logistic/linear baselines comfortably — but ~160 Elo is a clear, consistent deficit, not noise. The NODE-GAM study quantifies it cleanly on a medium-dataset suite: **GA2M models score ~0.81 normalized vs GAM ~0.46–0.53**, with full-complexity NODE/XGBoost around the GA2M level on most sets but pulling decisively ahead on Higgs and Year ([NODE-GAM, Chang et al. 2022](https://ar5iv.labs.arxiv.org/html/2106.01613)). So pairwise GAMs are *competitive but trailing* the unconstrained frontier by a small margin on typical data, and by a wide margin on a few high-interaction sets.

## (b) Oblivious (CatBoost) vs leaf-wise (LightGBM) — gap ≈ 0

This is the most encouraging axis for pattern-boost, because it isolates the *structural* cost of obliviousness from the *order-cap* cost. The evidence (research/01) is that **oblivious symmetric trees do not lose final-ensemble accuracy** — they trade a little per-tree bias for lower variance and recover it with more, lower-LR trees. On TabArena's tuned+ensembled leaderboard, **CatBoost (oblivious) and LightGBM (leaf-wise) are within a few rungs of each other** (LightGBM ~2nd, CatBoost ~4th among GBDTs), and CatBoost ranks *first* in the conventional-tuning regime ([TabArena](https://arxiv.org/html/2506.16791v4)). CatBoost's NeurIPS-2018 table shows mostly ≤2.4% logloss gaps, with its big wins (Amazon +17%) attributable to ordered target statistics, *not* the tree shape (research/01 §3). **Conclusion: obliviousness itself costs essentially nothing at the ensemble level.** Pattern-boost's structural penalty is therefore the depth-3 *order cap*, not the symmetric growth — and it can claw back CatBoost's categorical edge via the Fisher-sorted ordered-TS path (design §1) without breaking exactness.

## (c) Low-order fANOVA truncation vs full models — order-2→3 recovers a real slice

The cleanest order-ablation evidence is SIAN ([Enouen & Liu 2022](https://ar5iv.labs.arxiv.org/html/2209.09326)). Key findings, which map almost one-to-one onto pattern-boost's thesis:
- On most benchmarks the **best model is a 2- or 3-dimensional GAM**, not the full network — order-2/3 is frequently the bias-variance sweet spot, and higher orders *overfit* (e.g. Appliances Energy: order-1 best; Wine: plateau at order 2–3).
- **Going past order 3 rarely helps** and often hurts on medium data — the order-3 cap is mostly a ceiling you don't hit.
- The exceptions are explicit: **Higgs and Year** are "the only datasets where order ≤5 is insufficient to beat a DNN" — genuine high-order physics/audio structure. NODE-GAM independently flags **Higgs (+7.3%) and Year** as having "important higher-order interactions" beyond pairwise.

How much does order-2→3 specifically recover? No paper isolates exactly this, but the GAMI-Net/GAMI-Tree comparison ([Sun et al. 2023](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2305.15670)) shows order-2 GAMI models *matching or beating* XGBoost when interactions are ≤2-way, and only falling behind "in the presence of third-order interactions" — and even then, with **correlated predictors GAMI models can still beat XGBoost at order 3**, because XGBoost's greedy splits mis-handle correlation. This implies the order-3 layer is precisely the term that converts "trailing on third-order datasets" into "competitive," and that the *correlated-feature* regime (typical of insurance/tabular) actually favors the structured low-order model. My best estimate: **order-3 recovers roughly a third to two-thirds of the residual EBM→GBDT gap** on datasets where that gap stems from 3-way (not ≥4-way) structure — which, per SIAN, is most of them.

## Where the gap is ~0 vs large

- **Gap ≈ 0 (win or tie):** datasets dominated by main effects + low-order interactions — most business/tabular/insurance data, anything where SIAN's order-2/3 is the sweet spot, and *correlated-feature* problems where greedy unconstrained trees overfit interaction artifacts. Pattern-boost should also **beat EBM essentially everywhere** (strictly more capacity at order 3 plus full Newton splits), which is the actual milestone.
- **Gap large (lose):** datasets with genuine dense ≥4-way structure or extremely high-frequency/irregular target functions — Higgs, Year, and similar. Grinsztajn et al. note tabular targets are often "irregular," and where that irregularity is genuinely high-order, a 3rd-order cap *cannot represent it at all* — a bias ceiling, not a variance one ([Grinsztajn 2022](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2207.08815)).

## Realistic expected gap for pattern-boost on TabArena-like data

Reasoning from the anchors: EBM is **~160 Elo** below LightGBM. Pattern-boost should *strictly improve on EBM* — three independent gains stack: (1) exact **order-3** (recovering the largest single missing piece per SIAN/GAMI), (2) **full Newton split gain + exact leaf weights** vs EBM's tiny-LR cyclic stumps, (3) **CatBoost-grade categorical handling**. Each is worth real Elo. A defensible point estimate is that pattern-boost lands **roughly half to two-thirds of the way from EBM toward the GBDT frontier** — call it **~50–90 Elo behind the best GBDT on average**, i.e. clearly beating EBM/GA2M (the milestone) and "within striking distance" of XGBoost/LightGBM/CatBoost, *for the bulk of datasets*. The averaged number will be dragged down by the high-order minority (Higgs/Year-likes) where the cap costs 5–10%+. Two caveats temper optimism: a depth-3 cap is **more aggressive than CatBoost's depth-6** (per-tree weakness is stronger → needs the accelerated-boosting/fully-corrective-refit/multi-Newton levers in design §3 to hit tree-count parity), and **bagging/model-soup fixes variance but not the order-3 bias ceiling** — on truly high-order data, no amount of ensembling closes it.

**Net:** the gap is genuinely closable to "beat EBM, near-parity with black boxes" on most TabArena data; it is *not* closable on the ≥4-way-interaction minority, and honesty about that minority (AIM's stated risk) is the right posture.

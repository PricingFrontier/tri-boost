## Accuracy & model class

This section covers split/leaf-estimation refinements, linear/piecewise-linear leaves, DART, soft/neural oblivious-tree models (NODE/GRANDE/TEL/NODE-GAM), extra-trees randomization, accelerated/momentum & fully-corrective boosting, model-class hybrids (GBM+LR leaf transform, CANN/GLM-residual, stacking), and accuracy-oriented regularization. The unifying lens: pattern-boost is a depth-3 oblivious GBM whose trained ensemble decomposes **losslessly** into constant + 1D/2D/3D constant-cell lookup tables. Any technique here is judged on whether it (1) composes with one-shared-split-per-level oblivious trees, (2) preserves the exact ≤3rd-order **constant-cell** table form, and (3) actually buys accuracy/robustness/uncertainty for that price.

The headline result of this section: **almost every "better model class" idea that beats plain GBDT — linear leaves, soft/neural oblivious trees, NODE/GRANDE/TEL, GrowNet — buys its accuracy precisely by destroying either obliviousness or the constant-cell tables.** The ideas that are *safe* are the ones that touch only the boosting loop (momentum, DART, fully-corrective refit) or the leaf/split scoring math (Newton gain, leaf iterations, backtracking, accuracy regularizers) — and several of those are genuinely high value.

### Highest-value adoptions

#### `boost_from_average` — the only v1 entry, and it is load-bearing

Initializing F₀ at the loss-minimizing constant is not a generic convenience here: **the optimal constant IS the f₀ term of the fANOVA decomposition and the base rate of the rating table** ([CatBoost](https://catboost.ai/docs/en/references/training-parameters/common), [XGBoost intercept tutorial](https://xgboost.readthedocs.io/en/stable/tutorials/intercept.html)). It composes trivially with obliviousness (it's a scalar, not a tree) and preserves exactness by construction.

The spec must mandate four things or it silently forfeits its own benefit:
- **Score-space, exposure-weighted.** For Poisson with a log link and exposure offset the correct constant is F₀ = log(Σwᵢyᵢ / Σwᵢeᵢ), *not* log(mean y). Computing in response space or ignoring the offset corrupts the base-rate interpretation of f₀ as the deployable base premium.
- **Gradient-based generalization.** Gamma/Tweedie/Huber with a link have no closed-form constant; solve F₀ by one or a few Newton steps on the offset-weighted total loss (F₀ = −G/H using the trainer's existing g/h machinery) rather than hardcoding per-loss formulas. This mirrors what XGBoost ≥2.0 already does (one-step Newton for most objectives).
- **Keep F₀ as a separate scalar**, never absorbed into "tree 0" with a special learning rate of 1.0 (LightGBM's [issue #1514](https://github.com/microsoft/LightGBM/issues/1514) footgun) — so it lands cleanly in the intercept and the losslessness test stays clean.
- **Degenerate-input guards** (all-zero exposure, single-class target) to avoid −∞/NaN starts.

Interpretability note: with `boost_from_average` on, f₀ already carries the optimal constant, so purification moves *less* mass into f₀. This is harmless (the sum is still bit-for-bit exact) but means the reported intercept differs from a from-zero run — document that base score = optimal constant + purified residual intercept.

#### Leaf-estimation refinements (`leaf_estimation_iterations`, backtracking)

`score_function` and `leaf_estimation_method` are partly a re-statement of the project's existing core: pattern-boost should default to the **NewtonL2 split gain ΣG²/(H+λ)** for split *ranking* and `leaf_estimation_method=Newton` (w = −G/(H+λ)) for leaf *values* — these are XGB/LGBM parity and already on the v1 roadmap, and NewtonL2 being GPU-only in CatBoost is a CatBoost engineering limit, not a math one. Two clarifications worth pinning down: `score_function` ranks splits, `leaf_estimation_method` fills leaves — they are independent knobs the finder records conflated. Cosine/NewtonCosine are an *angular, scale-invariant* split score that is genuinely novel-to-evaluate but has no clean ablation evidence outside CatBoost's full regime, so treat it as a research A/B, not a v1 commitment.

The genuinely new accuracy lever is **`leaf_estimation_iterations > 1`**: take several Newton/gradient steps per fixed tree, re-deriving gradients at the updated leaf values each step. This sharpens leaves on the **non-quadratic pricing losses** (Gamma/Tweedie/MAE) that are pattern-boost's whole point, and only touches the 8 leaf values of an already-fixed oblivious tree — structure, the ≤3-feature property, and table form are untouched (**preserves exactness**, v1.5). Caveats: (a) **`Exact` buys nothing for v1** — it is a closed-form leaf optimum only for L1-type losses (MAE/Quantile/MAPE), contingent on shipping the optional quantile/L1 severity objective; (b) **do not adopt `Gradient` as default** — first-order leaves are strictly worse for non-quadratic losses; (c) verify the cost path — if g,h are recomputed from leaf-aggregated sums it is cheap, but exact per-leaf optima of arbitrary losses may need per-row re-evaluation per iteration, which is why CatBoost itself defaults to 1 for most regression.

**`leaf_estimation_backtracking`** (AnyImprovement default, or Armijo) is the natural companion: line-search the leaf-update step so it cannot worsen the loss. It only matters when `leaf_estimation_iterations > 1` and is pure value-level hardening for ill-conditioned losses (Tweedie with small variance power, MAE) — native, preserves exactness, v2.

#### `path_smooth` — credibility shrinkage for thin pricing cells

`path_smooth` shrinks each leaf toward its parent's value, weighted by sample count: `w_node = w·n/(n+ps) + w_parent·ps/(n+ps)` where `w = −G/(H+λ)` ([LightGBM](https://lightgbm.readthedocs.io/en/latest/Parameters.html), implement from the code form per [issue #5139](https://github.com/microsoft/LightGBM/issues/5139), not the old docs). For a depth-3 oblivious tree this regularizes the 8 leaves toward their depth-2/1 ancestors — a Bayesian posterior-mean with the parent as prior, exactly the regime of low-exposure rating cells (a rare postcode, a singleton vehicle group). It only changes leaf *values* (still 8 constants), so the ≤3rd-order table decomposition is preserved exactly (v1.5).

How-to-adopt subtleties: (1) "native" is overstated — it needs explicit construction of the recursive root-to-leaf parent path in the oblivious builder, not just a leaf post-step; (2) the prior is the ancestor split-node value, **not** a designed actuarial credibility complement, so treat `path_smooth` as a *foundation* for a proper credibility-shrinkage feature rather than the final form; (3) requires `min_data_in_leaf ≥ 2`; (4) test the interaction with monotone constraints and exposure-offset Newton weights — shrinkage toward a parent could in principle nudge a leaf across a monotonicity bound, so verify constraint enforcement happens *after* smoothing; (5) confirm the reconstructed-table == ensemble invariant still holds with smoothing on.

#### Inner/outer bagging — smoother tables + per-cell error bars

This is higher value for pattern-boost than for any black-box GBM. Train N outer bags (bootstrap resamples), each an independent oblivious ensemble; average their **exact tables** to get an exact averaged table set *plus a standard-error band per cell* — directly the credibility/confidence-on-relativities artifact actuaries want ([EBM/InterpretML](https://interpret.ml/docs/ebm.html), default 14 outer bags). The averaging is justified by purification linearity.

Three caveats the "fANOVA: none" verdict glosses: (1) **exact averaging requires a common merged-threshold union grid** — unlike EBM's fixed global pre-binning, pattern-boost picks thresholds per-tree, so bags disagree on cuts *and* on which feature triples appear; the averaged table set is the *union* across bags (denser), and SE/averaging must run on that aligned grid. (2) The per-cell std-deviations are an *annotation, not a fANOVA component* — variances are not additive like effects and are not purification-invariant, so compute them as "purify each bag, then take the spread," always tied to the chosen reference measure. (3) Cost is the binding constraint (~8–25× training time threatens the speed-parity bar) and the accuracy lift is marginal, so make it **opt-in / default-off** and sell it on uncertainty/credibility, not accuracy. `inner_bags` (row subsample within tree growth) is the cheaper, structure-neutral half and can ship independently.

#### Accelerated boosting — directly attacks the "weak per tree" risk

Oblivious depth-3 trees are deliberately weak per tree, so pattern-boost expects many low-lr trees. Nesterov-accelerated boosting (AGBM/AGB) attacks this head-on: O(1/m²) function-space convergence instead of O(1/m), reported as an order-of-magnitude *fewer trees* for the same error and much-reduced learning-rate sensitivity ([Lu et al. AISTATS'20](https://arxiv.org/abs/1903.08708), [Biau et al. 2019](https://arxiv.org/abs/1803.02042)). AGBM maintains three sequences (model f, Nesterov combination g, momentum h) with θₘ=2/(m+2) and fits a *second* tree per iteration to a corrected pseudo-residual to stop momentum error accumulating with inexact learners.

It is a pure boosting-loop modification: each tree is still a depth-3 oblivious table and the momentum combination g is a linear mix of ensembles, so it stays a sum of ≤3-feature functions and the LUT export is preserved (**fANOVA: none**). **Fewer trees means smaller, more readable tables** — a double win for the explainability mission. Marked v2/research because AGBM's 2-trees-per-iteration cost (~2× per iteration) and its tendency to overfit earlier (needs early stopping) must be benchmarked against the lighter Biau AGB before committing.

#### Fully-corrective leaf refit — the one "neural/soft" idea that is fully exact-safe

Standard GBM only re-fits the newest tree's leaves. **Fully-corrective boosting periodically re-optimizes the leaf values of ALL trees jointly** against the regularized loss, with structure frozen ([RGF](https://arxiv.org/pdf/1109.0887), [totally-corrective CGBoost](https://arxiv.org/pdf/1008.5188), [FCGB](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2004.00179)). This is the rare idea that the soft/neural literature (GrowNet's corrective step, NODE's joint training) gestures at while remaining **fully exact-compatible** for pattern-boost: structure is untouched (still ≤3 features/tree, axis-aligned, hard), only the 8 leaf scalars per tree are re-fit, so fANOVA decomposition and lossless tables survive bit-for-bit.

The implementation is clean for oblivious trees: with structure frozen the model is *linear in the leaf values*, and the design matrix is exactly the per-tree leaf-membership (one-hot of the 3-bit leaf code) — already computed during inference. A joint Newton/ridge (IRLS) solve over `#trees × 8` parameters corrects stagewise over-shrinkage and reduces tree count. It also composes perfectly with purification (refit, then purify). The per-round global solve is the cost (hence v2/research), so quantify the tree-count reduction vs. solve cost on pricing data before defaulting it on. **Note the GBM+LR leaf-transform ([He et al. ADKDD'14](https://quinonero.net/Publications/predicting-clicks-facebook.pdf)) is the same idea in disguise:** a GLM over leaf indicators is a re-weighting of the same tables — refit leaf values, re-purify, and the lossless decomposition holds. (Do *not* ship the leaf-one-hot as a feature for a *downstream black-box* stage — that reintroduces an opaque model and breaks the "tables ARE the predictor" thesis.)

#### DART — a known regularizer with non-unit-weight bookkeeping

DART drops a random subset of already-built trees when fitting each new one, then renormalizes (dropped + new trees rescaled so total contribution is preserved), combating the over-specialization that shrinkage alone doesn't fully cure ([Rashmi & Gilad-Bachrach AISTATS'15](https://proceedings.mlr.press/v38/korlakaivinayak15.pdf)). Each tree is still an independent depth-3 oblivious table, so the final ensemble is a sum of ≤3-feature tables and exactness holds (**fANOVA: none**, v2). The one wrinkle: the per-round normalization bakes **non-unit scale weights** into trees, which the table-accumulation step must fold into the final exported relativities (a closed-form fold-in) so the rating table stays clean. DART also slows training (no running-sum prediction buffer) and destabilizes early stopping — secondary to constraint/calibration work.

### Tricky compatibility calls

#### Linear / piecewise-linear leaves — preserves order ≤3, but **changes the table FORM**

This is the most important "looks safe, isn't quite" call in the section. Replacing constant leaves with a small linear model per leaf is **not** an exactness break in the order sense — if the leaf regressors are constrained to the tree's own ≤3 split features (the GBDT-PL "incremental feature selection," where a depth-d branch has ≤d regressors), the ensemble stays a sum of ≤3-feature functions and **fANOVA still truncates at 3rd order, losslessly** ([Shi et al. IJCAI'19](https://www.ijcai.org/proceedings/2019/0476.pdf), [arXiv:1802.05640](https://arxiv.org/abs/1802.05640)). The break is in **form**: each table cell becomes a piecewise-*linear* shape function (x ↦ a + bx) instead of a flat relativity. That is a deliberate, GAM-style change, not a corruption — but it discards the "deployable lookup grid" that is pattern-boost's product.

Two hard correctness traps make this **skip / research**, not v1.5:
- **The production LightGBM `linear_tree` is worse than the GBDT-PL variant for our purposes.** LightGBM fits leaf linear models over *all* numerical features on the branch path, which couples >3 features per tree and genuinely **breaks** order ≤3 (empirically visible as the [contributions-don't-sum bug #4002](https://github.com/microsoft/LightGBM)). Only the ≤3-feature GBDT-PL construction is native+order-preserving. The histogram changes too: each bin must accumulate a small Gram block (Σ h·xxᵀ, Σ g·x), not just scalar G,H.
- **Monotonicity is unsolved upstream.** LightGBM binds monotone constraints to *splits only, not leaf linear coefficients* — so a "monotone" pricing model can be non-monotone *within* a cell, which is unacceptable for regulated rating. A pricing-faithful variant would have to band/discretize the linear shape back to constants for deployment (discarding most of the claimed benefit) or solve a constrained-slope leaf fit (open work). `linear_lambda` only L2-regularizes coefficients and does not restore constant tables. Extrapolation is also unbounded (no slope clipping), risking extreme relativities at band tails.

Verdict: keep as **research**, gated on smooth extrapolation becoming a hard requirement; if pursued, restrict to the ≤3-feature GBDT-PL form, add slope clipping, and treat the output explicitly as a *piecewise-linear shape-function table*, a distinct deliverable from the bit-exact constant tables. A narrower carve-out worth noting but not recommending now: linear leaves on *main-effect-only* (single-feature) trees keep tables 1-D piecewise-linear and sidestep the interaction-tensor purification rewrite.

#### Soft / neural oblivious trees (NODE, GRANDE, TEL, NODE-GAM) — architecturally close, all break exactness

These are the soft analogues of pattern-boost and are worth understanding precisely because they fail in instructive ways:

- **[NODE](https://arxiv.org/abs/1909.06312)** literally *is* soft oblivious trees (α-entmax feature choice + entmoid splits, output = sum over 2^d leaves weighted by an outer product of soft level-choices). Two things break exactness: (1) splits stay **soft at inference** — each tree is a smooth blend, not a piecewise-constant ≤3-feature table; (2) **DenseNet stacking** feeds each layer's tree outputs as inputs to later layers, so a level-2 tree splitting on a level-1 tree's output is a high-order function of the raw features — effective interaction order *explodes* far past 3. Default depth is 6 with 2048 trees and no order cap. **Skip** — but it validates the oblivious *inductive bias* and could in principle pre-train (feature,threshold) tuples that seed a hard fit. Do not stack.

- **[GRANDE](https://arxiv.org/abs/2309.17130)** is SOTA-vs-GBDT on 19 tabular datasets and carries the **single most transferable trick in this group**: a *straight-through estimator* (hardmax feature choice + threshold rounding in the forward pass, soft gradients in the backward pass) that trains soft/global but keeps **hard axis-aligned splits at inference**. Axis-aligned + hard ⇒ exact tables. But GRANDE's own two distinguishing features break the invariant: (a) **instance-wise per-leaf weighting** (σ(w(x))·p(x)) makes each tree's contribution input-dependent in a non-additive way — *not* a fixed ≤3-feature table; (b) GRANDE trees are *not oblivious* (independent split per node). A pattern-boost-shaped research item would be: STE-trained, **oblivious**, **global** (not instance-wise) leaf values — which would keep exactness. That is novel and research-grade, not adopt-as-is.

- **[TEL](https://arxiv.org/abs/2002.07772)** contributes the cleanest soft→hard primitive: the **smooth-step** routing activation outputs *exactly* 0 and 1 outside a window of width γ, with γ→0 giving fully hard splits — a continuous, C¹ path from trainable-soft to deployable-hard, better-founded than the STE. As published it breaks exactness because (a) nodes are **oblique** (wᵀx, depends on >3 raw features) and (b) γ>0 leaves a fractional band. The research direction: *axis-aligned* smooth-step splits with γ annealed to 0 inside oblivious trees would preserve exact constant tables while gaining gradient trainability. **Skip** the technique, file the annealing idea as a differentiable-trees research seed.

- **[NODE-GAM](https://arxiv.org/abs/2106.01613)** is the strongest external validation that "oblivious tree = lookup table = explanation" is sound, and that the published field caps at order 2 (GAM/GA2M). It is soft and differentiable (not pattern-boost's hard greedy fit) and uses Lengerich purification to make GA2M tables identifiable. **Skip** for implementation, cite for motivation — pattern-boost's hard-GBM route to *exact order 3* is the differentiator. Beware the shared "oblivious" vocabulary: these are soft oblivious trees, a false friend to the hard ones.

- **[Frosst–Hinton soft trees](https://arxiv.org/abs/1711.09784)** and **[GrowNet](https://arxiv.org/abs/2002.07971)** are further from scope: oblique sigmoid gates (depend on all features) and dense MLP weak learners with penultimate-feature forwarding (unbounded interaction order) respectively. Both **skip**. GrowNet's one salvageable idea is its corrective step — already captured, exactly, by the fully-corrective leaf-refit above.

#### Non-symmetric growth & linear booster — definitionally out of scope (but keep as baselines)

`grow_policy=Depthwise/Lossguide` ([CatBoost](https://catboost.ai/docs/en/references/training-parameters/common)/[XGBoost](https://xgboost.readthedocs.io/en/stable/parameter.html)) and `gblinear` ([XGBoost](https://xgboost.readthedocs.io/en/stable/parameter.html)) **violate invariant (1) by definition** — per-node best splits touch >3 features and destroy the ≤3rd-order decomposition; a linear booster isn't a tree at all. **Skip as a mode**, but Depthwise/Lossguide/XGBoost/LightGBM remain *essential benchmark baselines*: AIM commits to honestly reporting where the order-3 cap loses against unrestricted GBMs. Two non-obvious notes: CatBoost's *own* `grow_policy=Depthwise` is non-oblivious and is **not** pattern-boost's per-level-but-shared-split search (ours is closer to CatBoost `SymmetricTree`); and `min_data_in_leaf` is meaningless under SymmetricTree's fixed 2^depth leaves — these are a structurally different model class ([CatBoost can't sum symmetric/non-symmetric models](https://catboost.ai/docs/en/references/training-parameters/common)). The only transferable fragment from `gblinear` is its coordinate-descent feature-selector (greedy/thrifty) as a possible per-level candidate *pre-ranking* heuristic on very wide data — a v2/research micro-optimization, not a reason to ship the booster.

#### Multi-output / vector-leaf trees — preserves exactness *per target*, becomes T table-sets

`multi_strategy=multi_output_tree` ([XGBoost](https://xgboost.readthedocs.io/en/stable/tutorials/multioutput.html)) shares one tree structure across targets, storing a *vector* leaf (w = −G/(H+λ) per target, block-diagonal Hessian). Vector leaves compose perfectly with oblivious depth-3 trees (shared split per level over pooled gradients, vector payload) and stay **constant-per-cell**, so the finder's `changes_table_form` is wrong — exactness is **preserved per target** (T aligned exact table sets sharing supports). The form only changes if leaves go non-constant, which this isn't. Genuinely useful for insurance (jointly modelling frequency+severity, or multiple perils/coverages sharing rating structure → a stack of T tables). But the per-tree weakness of oblivious trees *compounds* — one shared ≤3-feature support must serve all targets, so it only beats one-ensemble-per-target when targets are strongly correlated and prefer the same splits. Pricing blockers in XGBoost's current implementation: hist-only, dense-only, ~no Poisson/Gamma/Tweedie/offset support, and the block-diagonal Hessian ignores cross-target coupling. **Research** — a spike only after single-target pricing + purification are solid; the natural pattern-boost form is one shared oblivious structure emitting per-target leaf vectors → T purified table sets on common grids.

#### Extra-trees and stacking — exact but with sharp depth-3 edges

**`extra_trees`** ([LightGBM](https://lightgbm.readthedocs.io/en/latest/Parameters.html)) evaluates one random threshold per candidate feature instead of scanning all — a cheap variance-reduction knob that keeps constant-leaf depth-3 oblivious trees (preserves exactness, v2). The adaptation is non-trivial: it must be re-derived for the **per-level** loop (one random bin per candidate feature, summed gain over all 2^level leaves, best feature kept), *not* ported from LightGBM's per-node code. A single random border shared across a whole level is a *stronger, coarser* perturbation than LightGBM's per-node draw, so empirical variance-reduction differs and must be benchmarked, not assumed. Accuracy is explicitly dataset-dependent (helps noisy, hurts clean), so gate behind a flag, default off, and fix `extra_seed` for reproducible/defensible rating splits.

**Stacking/blending** ([sklearn](https://scikit-learn.org/stable/modules/ensemble.html#stacked-generalization)) is the section's most *conditional* verdict. A **linear blend of pattern-boost models is itself exact** — a sum of scaled ≤3-feature tables is again a sum of ≤3-feature tables, so an EBM-style "bagged pattern-boost" is a free variance/stability win that keeps exact tables (and yields uncertainty bands as the spread across bags). But the verdict **flips to breaks_exactness** the moment you (a) include any black-box base (deep GBM/NN → order >3) or (b) use a *non-linear* meta-learner over pattern-boost bases (sklearn's `StackingRegressor` defaults to exactly this — its docs use a `RandomForestRegressor` final estimator → composition inflates order even with all-pattern-boost bases). If pattern-boost ships any blend feature it **must** constrain the meta-learner to a linear (ideally non-negative, intercept-folded-into-f₀) combination and reject non-linear metas and black-box bases, or it silently destroys the product's reason to exist. Prefer the EBM-style outer-bag table-averaging framing (skips out-of-fold CV cost, same variance reduction, free error bars); the blended grid is the *union* of base thresholds, so tables grow denser — prune by purified variance afterward.

#### EBM cyclic round-robin & GAMI-Tree — validating cousins, not implementations

**EBM's `greedy_ratio`/cyclic round-robin** ([InterpretML](https://interpret.ml/docs/python/api/ExplainableBoostingRegressor.html)) is a GAM training *paradigm* (per-feature shape functions, one-feature-at-a-time boosting) fundamentally different from pattern-boost's full-data oblivious split search — **incompatible**, mentioned only to delimit scope. But two adjacent EBM ideas are worth separating out as their own (not-this-record) items: **`smoothing_rounds`** — initial highly-regularized rounds that establish main-effect shape before interactions — is portable to an oblivious schedule (restrict early rounds to depth-1/single-feature trees to seed mains, then allow depth-3) and preserves exactness; and EBM's tiny-lr + bagging recipe is the proven way to get stable shape functions for table-export quality.

**[GAMI-Tree / GAMI-Lin-Tree](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2309.02426)** is the nearest *published* validation of pattern-boost's entire approach (order-limited GBM → parse → purify → monotone tables), and its design lessons are directly importable even though the code is not (it is CART-greedy and capped at order 2): (1) use interaction **filtering** (FAST / model-based-tree) to choose which ≤3-feature trees to even build — caps table count and speeds search; (2) the explicit warning that *naive two-stage* fitting (all mains to convergence, then interactions on the residual) **fails to converge / mis-attributes mass when mains and interactions are correlated** — which argues for pattern-boost's *single joint boosting with order caps* over EBM-style staging, followed by one purification pass; (3) parse-then-purify with monotonicity on the *full* model. Be precise: the GAMI-**Lin**-Tree half uses linear/B-spline leaves, so it is `changes_table_form`, not "none." pattern-boost's extension from order-2 to *exact order-3* via oblivious trees is the novel contribution — mine these for the recipe, **research**.

#### Global surrogate — invert the relationship

A surrogate *of* pattern-boost would break exactness and is pointless (pattern-boost is already a glass-box). The real opportunity is the inverse: **pattern-boost can serve as a high-fidelity, order-3-exact surrogate FOR someone's black-box GBM/NN** — fit it to (X, blackbox(X)) and read its exact purified tables as an audited (R²<1-to-the-black-box, but internally exact) approximation ([Molnar IML](https://christophm.github.io/interpretable-ml-book/global.html)). That is a downstream positioning/use-case for the already-built learner, zero invariant-relevant engineering — **skip** as a build item, note as marketing.

---

### Summary of form-changing / exactness-breaking flags in this section

- **Changes table FORM (constant cells → non-constant shape functions), still order ≤3:** `linear_tree`/GBDT-PL linear leaves (with the ≤3-feature constraint), GAMI-Lin-Tree.
- **Breaks exactness (order >3 or non-additive composition):** production LightGBM `linear_tree` (all-path-feature leaves), `grow_policy=Depthwise/Lossguide`, `gblinear`, NODE/GRANDE/TEL/NODE-GAM/Frosst–Hinton/GrowNet (soft or oblique or feature-forwarding), non-linear-meta or black-box stacking, surrogate-of-pattern-boost.
- **Non-unit tree weights to fold into table export (exact but needs bookkeeping):** DART.
- **Preserves exactness but worth a clarification correction over the source verdicts:** multi-output vector-leaf trees (per-target exact, *not* changes_table_form), `path_smooth`/`leaf_estimation_iterations`/backtracking (leaf-value-only, exact), fully-corrective leaf refit (the only soft/neural-adjacent idea that is fully exact-safe).

The actionable v1/v1.5 set from this section is small and clean: **`boost_from_average`** (v1, the fANOVA intercept), **`leaf_estimation_iterations` + backtracking** and **`path_smooth`** (v1.5, leaf-value accuracy/credibility on non-quadratic pricing losses), and **inner/outer bagging** (v1.5, smooth tables + credibility error bars). The high-upside research bets — **accelerated boosting** (fewer trees → smaller tables), **fully-corrective refit** (sparser ensembles, fully exact), and the **STE/smooth-step soft-train-hard-infer** primitive (a differentiable oblivious variant) — all preserve the invariants and target pattern-boost's named weakness that depth-3 oblivious trees are weak per tree. Everything that promises a *better model class* by going soft, oblique, linear-leaf, or non-symmetric pays for it in exactly the currency pattern-boost cannot spend: the exact constant-cell ≤3rd-order tables.

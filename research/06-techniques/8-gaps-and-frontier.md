## Gaps & further research

- **ANOVA-BART (Bayesian Additive Regression Trees under the fANOVA uniqueness constraint)** — A Bayesian sum-of-trees model whose components are estimated under the same identifiability/uniqueness constraint tri-boost enforces, giving exact low-order decomposition *plus* full posterior credible intervals on every table cell. Directly relevant: it is a probabilistic cousin that yields per-cell uncertainty on the rating tables for free, where the catalogue's uncertainty methods (SGLB, NGBoost, PGBM) do not give table-cell posteriors. https://arxiv.org/abs/2510.00545 (and Park et al. 2024)

- **ANOVA-TPNN / ANOVA-NODE (identifiable Tensor-Product / oblivious-neural fANOVA nets)** — Neural fANOVA models that *guarantee a unique decomposition* via tensor-product bases (ICML 2025), solving the instability that plagues GAMI-Net/NODE-GAM. Worth studying as the theoretical reference for *why* tri-boost's purification is identifiable and as a distillation target/source; ANOVA-NODE is literally oblivious-tree-shaped. https://arxiv.org/abs/2502.15215 , https://openreview.net/forum?id=Xy1Lf7uR9H

- **InstaSHAP (variational GAM↔SHAP equivalence; train a purified GAM that emits exact Shapley in one forward pass)** — ICLR 2025 result formalizing that a purified additive model *is* the Shapley computation, so tri-boost can output exact SHAP in O(1) from its purified tables with no TreeSHAP pass at all. Sharper and more recent than the catalogue's "SHAP from fANOVA = equal-split" note. https://arxiv.org/abs/2502.14177

- **Treeffuser (conditional diffusion with GBT-estimated score function)** — NeurIPS 2024 nonparametric probabilistic head: GBTs estimate a diffusion score, giving calibrated multimodal/skewed predictive distributions without a parametric (Tweedie/Gamma) assumption. A drop-in distributional alternative for claim severity where the parametric LSS family misfits; the score-net could in principle be oblivious. https://arxiv.org/abs/2406.07658

- **Supervised Score-Based Modeling by Gradient Boosting** — Companion 2024 line generalizing Treeffuser's score-boosting; relevant as the design space for "distributional output from a boosted-tree score model" that tri-boost could adopt while keeping trees oblivious. https://arxiv.org/pdf/2411.01159

- **TabResFlow (normalizing-spline-flow head for univariate tabular regression)** — 2025 method giving flexible, calibrated 1-D predictive densities via a monotone spline flow on top of a tabular learner — a more expressive distributional head than fixed Gamma/Tweedie for severity, and the monotone-spline output is compatible with monotone pricing constraints. https://arxiv.org/pdf/2508.17056

- **Multicalibration-as-boosting and its convergence theory (Globus-Harris 2023; Jin 2025; Tax 2026)** — Boosting framework that enforces calibration *simultaneously across many overlapping subgroups* (not just the global balance the catalogue's autocalibration step gives). Directly upgrades tri-boost's "multicalibration/multibalance" bullet from a name to an algorithm with finite-sample/iteration guarantees, and composes with boosting natively. https://proceedings.mlr.press/v202/globus-harris23a/globus-harris23a.pdf , https://arxiv.org/pdf/2602.06773

- **Calibration Bands for the Exponential Dispersion Family (Tweedie/Poisson/Gamma)** — 2025 actuarial method giving honest confidence *bands* on the reliability/A-vs-E diagram for EDF responses, so you can statistically test whether a pricing model is auto-calibrated rather than eyeball it. Fills the "is the balance fix actually significant?" gap in the catalogue's reliability-diagram bullet. https://arxiv.org/pdf/2503.18896

- **Distribution-Free (conformal) Inference for LightGBM/GLM with Tweedie loss** — 2025 paper building conformal predictive intervals specifically around a Tweedie-loss GBM for pricing — the missing concrete instantiation of "conformal for Tweedie/pricing" (catalogue lists CQR/MAPIE generically but not the Tweedie-specific construction). https://arxiv.org/html/2507.06921v1

- **Conformal prediction for frequency-severity / future-claims regression (Hong-Martin lineage, 2023-2025)** — Conformal machinery tailored to the two-stage frequency×severity workflow and to predicting *aggregate* future claims, giving distribution-free coverage on the compound loss rather than on a single head. Relevant to tri-boost's frequency-severity export path. https://arxiv.org/pdf/2503.03659 , https://arxiv.org/pdf/2307.13124

- **Conditional-coverage diagnostics for conformal prediction** — 2025 tooling to *detect* where marginal conformal intervals violate local/conditional coverage (e.g. for thin risk cells) — a validation layer tri-boost needs before shipping conformal intervals on a rating table. https://arxiv.org/pdf/2512.11779

- **HyperDM-style single-model epistemic+aleatoric disentanglement** — NeurIPS 2024 approach to split epistemic vs aleatoric uncertainty from one model; conceptually maps onto SGLB virtual ensembles but gives an explicit decomposition target. Worth contrasting with the catalogue's "data vs knowledge uncertainty" to see if oblivious virtual ensembles can recover the same split. https://neurips.cc/virtual/2024/poster/94833

- **Extended functional links for unified Zero-Inflated Tweedie boosting (So-Valdez 2024; 2025 follow-ups)** — Goes beyond the catalogue's generic ZITw bullet: a *specific* learned functional relationship between the zero-inflation probability q and the Tweedie mean μ, shown to beat classic two-part models, with CatBoost reference implementation. Concrete loss/link to implement for pricing. https://arxiv.org/html/2406.16206v2

- **TreeLUT (quantized GBDT → pure-LUT hardware/sub-microsecond inference)** — FPGA '25 method compiling a quantized GBDT into lookup tables with 2-4 bit quantization at ~0-1% accuracy loss. Tri-boost's depth-3 oblivious trees are *already* 8-entry LUTs, so this is the natural deployment-compilation target beyond Treelite/lleaves and validates the LUT-sum inference direction with hardware numbers. https://arxiv.org/abs/2501.01511

- **CatBoost SIMD/RVV prediction vectorization (RISC-V, 2024)** — Primary-source engineering paper on hand-vectorizing CatBoost's *oblivious-tree* prediction loop (the exact branch-free bit-index gather tri-boost wants), with concrete RVV kernels — direct reference for the inference-layer invariant. https://arxiv.org/abs/2405.11062

- **GRANDE / "Learning Tree-Based Models with Gradient Descent" (2024-2026, straight-through hard axis-aligned trees + instance-wise leaf weighting)** — The catalogue lists GRANDE, but the 2026 successor paper generalizes the straight-through *tree-routing* and parallel parameter optimization; its routing formulation is the cleanest published recipe for gradient-training *oblivious* hard trees end-to-end (an alternative to greedy per-level search) while preserving exact hard regions. https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.11117

- **Tensor-network exact Shapley / SHAP-interactions (2025)** — Provably tractable exact Shapley values and interaction indices via tensor-network contraction; relevant as an alternative exact-attribution engine and as theory connecting the fANOVA tensor structure to Shapley-Taylor/Faith-Shap on tri-boost's tables. https://arxiv.org/html/2510.22138v1

- **TabArena living benchmark + TabM / RealMLP (2024-2025 "GBDTs no longer strictly win")** — TabM, LightGBM and RealMLP now top a maintained tabular leaderboard; RealMLP ships strong *meta-tuned GBDT defaults*. Two takeaways for tri-boost: (a) a credible, continuously-updated parity yardstick to benchmark against, and (b) ready-to-use tuned default hyperparameter sets to seed v1 defaults. https://arxiv.org/html/2506.16791v4 , https://arxiv.org/abs/2407.04491

- **TabPFN v2 (Nature 2025 tabular foundation model with piecewise-constant predictive distribution)** — In-context-learning model giving a full posterior-predictive density (incl. multimodal) on small data with zero training. Relevant as (a) a strong small-portfolio accuracy/uncertainty baseline tri-boost must justify itself against, and (b) a distillation source whose piecewise-constant output is naturally table-shaped. https://www.nature.com/articles/s41586-024-08328-6 (paper); https://arxiv.org/abs/2502.17361 (analysis/extensions)

- **Discrimination-free pricing with privatized/unobserved sensitive attributes (2024-2025)** — Extends the catalogue's marginalization-based discrimination-free bullet to the realistic case where the protected attribute is *privatized or only partially observed*, with statistical guarantees — the regime insurers actually operate in. https://arxiv.org/html/2504.11775

- **Intersectional fairness via distance-covariance regularization (JdCov/CCdCov, 2025)** and **multi-objective fairness-aware pricing (2025)** — In-processing penalties using joint/concatenated distance covariance to control dependence on *multiple, mixed-type* protected attributes at once (guards against fairness gerrymandering), plus a Pareto multi-objective framing of accuracy-vs-fairness. More general than the single-attribute dependence penalty in the catalogue. https://arxiv.org/pdf/2509.08163 , https://arxiv.org/pdf/2512.24747

- **Probabilistic-GBM review for claim frequency & severity (2024-2025 survey, PMC/arXiv)** — A pricing-specific head-to-head of NGBoost / PGBM / cyc-GBM / XGBoostLSS on frequency and severity, with calibration metrics — the empirical reference for *choosing which distributional head to ship* for each pricing objective, which the catalogue lists individually but never compares. https://arxiv.org/html/2412.14916v2

- **Identifiable purification under dependence / "GAM↔SHAP variational" lens as a unifying check** — Bordt-von Luxburg (n-Shapley) plus Enouen-Liu (InstaSHAP) plus Park et al. now jointly establish that a *purified* order-n additive model is simultaneously the unique fANOVA decomposition, the n-Shapley/Faith-Shap explanation, and the Möbius transform. This is the rigorous theoretical backbone tri-boost should cite to claim its 3rd-order tables ARE the exact Shapley/Faith-Shap up to order 3 — go beyond restating the equal-split heuristic. https://arxiv.org/abs/2209.04012 , https://arxiv.org/abs/2203.00870
